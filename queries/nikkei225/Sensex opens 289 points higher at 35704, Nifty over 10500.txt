New Delhi: Indian indices opened on a positive note on Thursday with Nifty above 10500. The Sensex opened 289.72 points or 0.82% higher at 35704.17, and the Nifty was up 84.20 points or 0.81% at 10514.20. About 796 shares have advanced, 274 shares declined, while 34 shares are unchanged.
The domestic indices rose more than half a per cent in early trade today, tracking gains from their Asian peers. The S&P BSE Sensex was up 250 points at 35,660 levels and the Nifty50 index reclaimed the 10,500-mark. Nifty Media gains over 1%, Nifty PSU bank extends gain to two days in a row. Top gainers and Top losers:
Mahindra & Mahindra, ONGC, and IndusInd Bank (all up 2%), Titan were the top gainers in the Sensex pack. Top losers in the pack today were Hindustan Unilever and Tech Mahindra. The trend among all Nifty sectoral indices was positive, led by Nifty Bank index which was up 1 per cent.
Both Indian benchmark indices were trading higher in the pre-opening session. At 09:03 am, the Sensex was up 169.06 points or 0.48% at 35583.51, and the Nifty was up 55.70 points or 0.53% at 10485.70.
On Wall Street, the S&P 500 gained 0.5 per cent and the Nasdaq 0.95 per cent higher to kick off the third quarter. On the other hand, the Dow Jones fell 0.3 per cent. Stocks in the Asia Pacific began on a positive note in early Thursday morning trade. The Japanese Nikkei 225 rose 0.39% with shares of conglomerate Softbank Group jumping 2%. The Topix index also added 0.63% while South Korea’s Kospi gained 0.78%.
Australia’s S&P/ASX 200 rose 0.44%, the MSCI Asia ex-Japan index traded 0.16% higher. The Stoxx 600 closed up by about 0.2% whilst the gains in the broader market were capped. German blue-chip DAX index slipped 0.5%
Oil prices dipped on Thursday after the US recorded its biggest one-day spike in coronavirus cases and California reimposed some lockdown measures. The surge in COVID-19 cases stoked worries that a resurgence will stall the recovery in fuel demand. While U.S. West Texas Intermediate (WTI) crude futures fell 10 cents, or 0.3%, to $39.72 a barrel, Brent crude futures eased 6 cents, or 0.1%, to $41.97 a barrel, after rising 1.8% in the previous session.
