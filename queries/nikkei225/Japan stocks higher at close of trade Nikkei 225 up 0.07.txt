Japan stocks higher at close of trade; Nikkei 225 up 0.07%
© Reuters.  Japan stocks higher at close of trade; Nikkei 225 up 0.07%
Investing.com – Japan stocks were higher after the close on Thursday, as gains in the Paper & Pulp , Railway & Bus and Real Estate sectors led shares higher.
At the close in Tokyo, the Nikkei 225 rose 0.07%.
The best performers of the session on the Nikkei 225 were Daiichi Sankyo Co., Ltd. (T: 4568 ), which rose 4.34% or 308.0 points to trade at 7400.0 at the close. Meanwhile, Subaru Corp (T: 7270 ) added 2.64% or 70.5 points to end at 2740.5 and Suzuki Motor Corp. (T: 7269 ) was up 2.50% or 118.0 points to 4832.0 in late trade.
The worst performers of the session were Taiyo Yuden Co., Ltd. (T: 6976 ), which fell 3.29% or 120.0 points to trade at 3525.0 at the close. IHI Corp. (T: 7013 ) declined 3.09% or 87.0 points to end at 2732.0 and CyberAgent Inc (T: 4751 ) was down 3.08% or 120.0 points to 3770.0.
Falling stocks outnumbered advancing ones on the Tokyo Stock Exchange by 2173 to 1389 and 227 ended unchanged.
The Nikkei Volatility , which measures the implied volatility of Nikkei 225 options, was unchanged 0.00% to 14.36.
Crude oil for February delivery was up 0.67% or 0.39 to $58.20 a barrel. Elsewhere in commodities trading, Brent oil for delivery in March rose 0.61% or 0.39 to hit $64.39 a barrel, while the February Gold Futures contract fell 0.07% or 1.05 to trade at $1552.95 a troy ounce.
USD/JPY was up 0.08% to 109.97, while EUR/JPY rose 0.07% to 122.61.
The US Dollar Index Futures was up 0.02% at 96.982.
