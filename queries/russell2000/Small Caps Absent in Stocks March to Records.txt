Generally considered riskier, small-company shares have stalled amid weaker economic data, recession fears
The S&P 500 has surged 22% this year, setting 16 record closes along the way. Meanwhile, the Russell 2000 index of small-cap stocks has been left behind.
That index is up 18% in 2019 but is still 8.7% below its high from August 2018, following a brutal selloff last fall.
