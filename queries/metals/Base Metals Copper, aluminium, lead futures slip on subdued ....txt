New Delhi: Lead prices eased by 0.29 per cent to Rs 153.80 per kg in futures trade on Friday as investors trimmed their holdings amid subdued demand at the spot market.
On the Multi Commodity Exchange , lead for delivery in this month was trading lower by 45 paise, or 0.29 per cent, to Rs 153.80 per kg in a business turnover of 2,192 lots.
Marketmen said, a weak trend in base metals at the domestic spot market due to slackened demand from battery-makers mainly exerted pressure on lead futures here.
Aluminium prices fell 0.18 per cent to Rs 140.85 per kg in futures trade on Friday as speculators cut down their positions taking negative cues from the spot market.
On the Multi Commodity Exchange, aluminium for delivery in August declined by 25 paise, or 0.18 per cent, to Rs 140.85 per kg in a business turnover of 662 lots.
Analysts said cutting down of positions by traders owing to slackened demand from consuming industries mainly weighed on aluminium prices here.
Copper prices on Friday moved down by 0.52 per cent to Rs 445.95 per kg as speculators cut down their holdings amid a weak trend in base metals at the domestic market.
On the Multi Commodity Exchange, copper to be delivered in August contracts eased by Rs 2.35, or 0.52 per cent, to Rs 445.95 per kg in a business turnover of 3,517 lots.
Analysts said subdued demand at domestic physical market led to fall in copper prices here.
Nickel prices rose 1.2 per cent in futures trade on Friday as speculators raised their bets amid spot demand.
On the Multi Commodity Exchange, nickel for delivery in August gained Rs 6.50, or 1.2 per cent, to Rs 1,138.40 per kg in a business turnover of 19,656 lots.
Increase in domestic demand from alloy-makers and firmness in base metals at the spot market mainly influenced nickel prices here, analysts said.
Zinc prices eased 0.11 per cent to Rs 184.20 per kg in futures trade Friday as speculators cut down positions, taking weak cues from the spot market.
On the Multi Commodity Exchange, zinc for delivery in current month declined 20 paise, or 0.11 per cent, to Rs 184.20 per kg in a business turnover of 3,369 lots.
Marketmen said weakness in metals in the domestic spot market owing to slackened demand from consuming industries mainly influenced prices.
