New Delhi: Copper futures traded higher by 90 paise to Rs 447.80 per kg in futures trade on Thursday after speculators built fresh positions amid a firm trend overseas.
On the Multi Commodity Exchange , copper contracts for September delivery rose by 90 paise, or 0.2 per cent, to Rs 447.80 per kg in a business turnover of 2,721 lots.
Copper contracts for October delivery traded up by 65 paise, or 0.14 per cent, to Rs 451.55 per kg in a business turnover of 154 lots.
In international market, the metal was quoting higher by 0.02 per cent to $261.35 per pound in New York.
Zinc prices rose by 40 paise to Rs 184.10 per kg in futures market on Thursday due to high demand in the spot market.
On the Multi Commodity Exchange, zinc contracts for September delivery traded higher by 40 paise, or 0.22 per cent, to Rs 184.10 per kg in a business turnover of 1,566 lots.
Similarly, the metal contracts for October delivery edged up by 30 paise, or 0.16 per cent, to Rs 186.90 per kg in 69 lots.
Market analysts said fresh positions created by participants on the back of rise in demand from consuming industries mainly attributed to rise in prices.
Lead prices traded higher by 20 paise at Rs 154.40 per kg in futures trade on Thursday as participants built up fresh positions driven by pick-up in demand at the spot market.
On the Multi Commodity Exchange, lead contracts for September delivery edged higher by 20 paise, or 0.13 per cent, to Rs 154.40 per kg in a business volume of 785 lots.
Similarly, the metal contracts for October delivery traded higher by 35 paise, or 0.23 per cent, at Rs 155.10 per kg in 12 business lots.
Market analysts said fresh positions created by traders due to upsurge in demand from battery makers in physical market mainly led to rise in lead prices.
Nickel prices edged up by Rs 3.50 to Rs 1,231.30 per kg in futures trade on Thursday as investors raised their positions driven by firm demand from alloy-makers in the spot market.
On the Multi Commodity Exchange, nickel contracts for September delivery gained Rs 3.50, or 0.29 per cent, to Rs 1,231.30 per kg in a business turnover of 4,965 lots.
Similarly, the metal for delivery in October traded up by Rs 4.40, or 0.36 per cent, at Rs 1,229.20 per kg in a business turnover of 378 lots.
Increase in demand at spot markets from alloy-makers supported price rise in nickel futures, analysts said.
