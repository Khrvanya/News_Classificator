New Delhi: Lead prices drifted lower by 0.19 per cent to Rs 155.75 per kg in futures market on Friday as speculators cut bets, taking negative cues from spot market.
On the Multi Commodity Exchange , lead for delivery in October fell by 30 paise, or 0.19 per cent, to Rs 155.75 per kg in 436 lots.
Likewise, the metal for delivery in November shed 40 paise, or 0.25 per cent, to Rs 156.50 per kg in 774 lots.
Analysts said off-loading of positions by participants owing to slackened demand from consuming industries in the physical market, mainly influenced lead prices at futures trade.
Copper futures traded 0.25 per cent lower at Rs 437.60 per kg in futures trade on Friday after speculators reduced their exposure.
On Multi Commodity Exchange, copper contracts for October delivery fell by Rs 1.10, or 0.25 per cent, to Rs 437.60 per kg in 1,004 lots.
Similarly, copper contracts for November delivery traded down by 95 paise, or 0.21 per cent, at Rs 443.35 per kg in 1,538 lots.
Globally, copper traded 0.02 per cent lower at $5,880 per tonne on the London Metal Exchange.
Nickel futures declined by 40 paise at Rs 1,220.80 per kg on Friday due to reducing of positions by speculators amid sluggish demand at the spot market.
On the Multi Commodity Exchange, nickel contracts for October delivery fell by 40 paise, or 0.03 per cent, to Rs 1,220.80 per kg with a business turnover of 1,755 lots.
Similarly, nickel contracts for November delivery fell by Re 1, or 0.08 per cent, to Rs 1,204.70 per kg in 4,599 lots.
Analysts said subdued demand at the spot market mainly led to the fall in nickel futures prices here.
Zinc futures traded lower by Rs 1.05 to Rs 184.70 per kg on Friday as speculators reduced exposure at the spot market.
At the Multi Commodity exchange, zinc contracts for October slipped 0.57 per cent, or Rs 1.05, to Rs 184.70 per kg in a business turnover of 574 lots.
For November contracts, zinc futures reduced by Rs 1.15, or 0.61 per cent, to Rs 186.95 per kg in a business turnover of 1,732 lots.
Cutting down of bets by speculators mainly led to fall in price of zinc futures, traders said.
