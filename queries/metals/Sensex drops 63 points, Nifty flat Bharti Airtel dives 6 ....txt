The benchmark Sensex ended marginally lower, after volatile trade in the last leg of today's session. Gains in metal stocks--JSW Steel, Jindal Steel, Tata Steel and Hindalco--were offset by steep falls in Bharti Airtel and IT heavyweights--Tata Consultancy Services (TCS), Infosys and Tech Mahindra. Sell-off in Bajaj Finance, Bajaj Finserve and Sun Pharmaceutical also dragged down the key index.
While the Sensex declined 63.39 points, or 0.2%, to 30,609.30, the Nifty 50 index settled nearly flat at 9,029.05.
Among banks, IDFC First Bank was the biggest Nifty gainer, rising nearly 6%, after reporting profit for January-March. HDFC Bank and IndusInd Bank also added to the bull run.
Eicher Motors surged 6.5% on Nifty 50, followed by JSW Steel, Titan Company, UltraTech Cement and IndusInd Bank, while Bharti Airtel, Bajaj Finserve, Tata Consultancy Services, Bajaj Finance and Sun Pharmaceutical led the losses.
Japan led the equity advance in Asia as the world’s third-largest economy reopened, and shares rose in Hong Kong, which showed signs of stabilising after the weekend unrest. Japan's Nikkei 225 ended 2.5% higher, while Hong Kong's Hang Seng and Korea's Kospi rose nearly 2%. Shanghai composite index in China was up 1%.

What is driving cement prices higher?
Cement maker Birla Corporation shares vaulted 12%. The stock followed the trend of peers whose shares rose on the back of decent results in the March quarter. ACC, Ambuja Cements, UltraTech Cement and Shree Cement are up 7-13% since the lockdown was partially eased on 21st April.
What is driving this optimism amid macroeconomic gloom? To begin with, investors are positively surprised by the sustained increase in cement prices in May. A report by Emkay Global Financial Services Ltd pointed out, “Our channel checks indicate that cement prices have been increased by Rs20-30/bag in the North, Central and West markets; Rs20-50/bag in the East markets……and Rs40-90/bag in the South region." The pan-European STOXX 600 rose 1% by 0708 GMT after hitting its highest level since March 10. Returning from a bank holiday, UK's FTSE 100 surged 1.9% as Prime Minister Boris Johnson said Britain will reopen thousands of high street shops, department stores and shopping centres next month.
