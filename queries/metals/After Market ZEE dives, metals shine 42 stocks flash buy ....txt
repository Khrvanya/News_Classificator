BSE flagship Sensex hit a record high of 40,931 during the day, but closed at 40,889, adding 530 points. It was also its all-time closing high. NSE barometer Nifty closed just 24 points shy of its all-time high at 12,079, jumping 164.60 points.
"News that the US and China will conclude a deal by next month lifted sentiment across the globe. Reconstruction of Sensex indices due on December 23 and new developments over divestment to curb fiscal deficit provided confidence to the Indian market. Expectations of further rate cuts by RBI boosted the rate-sensitive stocks," said Vinod Nair, Head of Research at Geojit Financial Services.
Telecom operator Bharti Airtel soared 7.2 per cent to Rs 451.30 on BSE after losses for three consecutive sessions. Since posting its Q2 results on November 14, the stock has jumped 24.5 per cent. For Q2, the company posted a net loss of Rs 23,045, as it provided for its statutory dues.
Zee Entertainment shares dived after the promoters of the broadcaster sold a large stake in the company, as per block deals data released on Friday. Promoters were looking to sell up to 16.5 per cent in the company. The stock slumped 5.33 per cent to Rs 339 on NSE on Monday.
Metal stocks rejoiced after a US official said over the weekend that an initial trade agreement was still possible by the end of the year. Jindal Steel was the biggest gainer in the pack, advancing 6.55 per cent to Rs 153.65 on NSE. Tata Steel was up 5.25 per cent to Rs 421 while Hindustan Copper climbed 5.23 per cent to Rs 42.25. The Nifty Metal index added 3.09 per cent to 2,598.95.
Shares of Ashoka Buildcon climbed 3.43 per cent to Rs 96.45 in Monday’s trade after an arm Ashoka Concessions received a Letter of Award (LOA) from NHAI for a highway project in Telangana worth Rs 1,000 crore.
Media was the sole sectoral loser on NSE dragged by losses in Zee Entertainment that dived 5.33 per cent to Rs 339. Network18, down 4.49 per cent to Rs 29.80 and Radio City, down 3.18 per cent to Rs 28.95 were other big losers from the sector. Nifty Media index slipped 1.63 per cent to 1,946.35.
Broader market indices also ended the day with healthy gains. Nifty Midcap was up 1.11 per cent to 17,054 while Nifty Smallcap added 0.69 per cent to 5,741. Nifty 500 advanced 1.29 per cent to 9,804.
The force of Monday’s rally was such that just two stocks—ONGC and YES Bank—in the 30-share pack registered losses. HDFC was the biggest contributor adding 96 points while Bharti Airtel and Axis Bank NSE 1.24 % chipped in with 56 and 50 points, respectively. Other major contributors were RIL, Kotak Mahindra Bank NSE 0.13 % and IndusInd Bank NSE -1.54 % .
Bharti Airtel was the most active security in terms of transacted value on NSE. It was followed by Indiabulls Housing Finance, ICICI Bank NSE -0.51 % , Tata Steel and YES Bank. In terms of volume, Vodafone Idea topped the charts whereas YES Bank, Indiabulls Housing Finance, Dish TV NSE 1.18 % and Uttam Value Steels.
As many as 42 stocks gave ‘buy’ signal as they crossed MACD Signal line during the session. They include Jindal Steel and Power, Tata Steel, Axis Bank, HDFC, JK Paper, Tata Chemicals, Divi’s Lab, Inox Wind and DCB Bank, among others. When the MACD falls below the signal line, it is a bearish signal that means it may be time to sell. Conversely, when the MACD rises above the signal line, the indicator gives a bullish signal, suggesting the price is likely to experience upward momentum.
