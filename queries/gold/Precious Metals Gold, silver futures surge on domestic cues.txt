New Delhi: Gold prices were up 0.98 per cent to Rs 38,262 per 10 gram in futures trade on Thursday as speculators extended exposure amid firm domestic cues.
On the Multi Commodity Exchange , gold contracts for December delivery surged by Rs 370, or 0.98 per cent, to Rs 38,262 per 10 gram in a business turnover of 2,579 lots.
The precious metal to be delivered in October traded up by Rs 503, or 1.35 per cent, to Rs 37,814 per 10 gram in a business turnover of 20 lots.
Analysts said raising of bets by participants taking positive cues from domestic markets mainly pushed up gold prices at futures trade here.
Globally, however, the yellow metal prices were trading lower by 0.25 per cent to $1,504.10 an ounce in New York.
Silver prices rose by Rs 758 at Rs 45,610 per kg in futures trade on Thursday as participants widened their bets despite a sluggish trend overseas.
On the Multi Commodity Exchange, silver contracts for December delivery gained Rs 758, or 1.69 per cent, to Rs 45,610 per kg in a business turnover of 4,473 lots.
Similarly, the white metal for delivery in March next year traded higher by Rs 799, or 1.75 per cent, to Rs 46,529 per kg in 199 lots.
Analysts said widening of positions by participants amid firm demand at physical markets influenced silver prices here.
In the international market, silver was down 0.19 per cent at $17.65 an ounce in New York.
