Gold futures tallied a fourth consecutive gain on Tuesday , buoyed by a decline in the dollar and weakness in the global economy, even as U.S. benchmark stock indexes climbed. June gold gcm20 rose $7.50, or 0.4%, to settle at $1,768.90 an ounce after trading as high as $1,788.80. Those were the highest settlement and intraday levels for a most-active contract since October 2012, according to FactSet data.
