( Kitco News ) - Yamana Gold (NYSE: AUY ) said Monday that it sold shares and  warrants of Ross Beaty's Equinox Gold (TSX: EQX ) for net proceeds of C$201  million (US$144.28 million).
Yamana entered into an agreement with Stifel GMP and Cormark  Securities to sell 12 million units at a price of C$10.00 per unit to qualified  purchasers, for gross proceeds to Yamana of C$120 million. The dealers  committed to purchase from Yamana any unsold units at a price of C$10.00 per  unit on closing, subject to customary conditions for “bought deal” financings.  Each unit consists of one common share of Equinox Gold owned by Yamana and  one-half of a common share purchase warrant of Yamana.
Yamana said it intends to use the net proceeds of the sale  transaction for general corporate purposes.
In a research note, Haywood said Mubadala and Ross Beaty are  now the largest shareholders of Equinox, holding 11.5% and 8.9% respectively.
Equinox has six producing gold mines with a plan for about  700,000 ounces of gold production in 2020.
