(Reuters) - Canada’s main stock index rose in early trading on Monday as hopes of a stimulus-led economic rebound offset worries about a resurgence in global coronavirus cases.
* At 9:30 a.m. ET (13:30 GMT), the Toronto Stock Exchange’s S&P/TSX composite index was up 160.1 points, or 1.03%, at 15,756.85.
