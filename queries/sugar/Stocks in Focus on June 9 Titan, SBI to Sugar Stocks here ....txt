The Sensex and the Nifty ended with small gains after a volatile session on Monday, June 8, 2020. Profit selling emerged at higher levels after the recent run-up in share prices. The barometer S&P BSE Sensex rose 83.34 points or 0.24% at 34,370.58. The Nifty 50 index added 25.30 points or 0.25% at 10,167.45. But certain stocks came in news after the market was closed. These stocks can impact the indices when it reopens on Tuesday, June 9, 2020. List of such five stocks:
Titan/ PVR/ Inox Leisure: They are the companies that reported their financial results of March quarter FY20 between on Friday, June 8, 2020, after the market hours. Find details:
Titan: Jewellery maker Titan on Monday posted a year-on-year growth of 21% in net profit to Rs 356 crore for the quarter ended March 31. It posted a profit of Rs 295 crore in the same period last year. Analysts at Zee Business had projected the figure at Rs 265 crore for Q4FY20. Revenue from operations declined 5% to Rs 4,429 crore from Rs 4,672 crore posted last year. EBITDA stood at Rs 604 crore against Rs 455 crore of last year, while margin grew to 13.6% against 9.7%. Higher Gross Margins and lower Ad spend boosts margins. Margins high also due to IND-AS 116. EBITDA grew due to significant compression of cost in anticipation of the disruption.
PVR Ltd: Multiplex chain operator PVR on Monday reported a consolidated net loss of Rs 76 crore for the fourth quarter ended March 2020. The company had posted a net profit of Rs 46.75 crore in the January-March quarter a year ago. It posted a loss because the film exhibition business was impacted because of COVID-19 pandemic. Revenue from operations during the quarter under review declined 21% to Rs 662 crore. It was Rs 837.6 crore in the corresponding quarter a year ago. EBITDA declined 64.9% to Rs 59.4 crore from Rs 169 crore posted a year ago. Margins declined to 9% from 20.2% posted a year-ago period. Besides, the board of PVR has approved its plan to raise Rs 300 crore through a rights issue.
INOX Leisure Ltd: Multiplex chain operator Inox Leisure on Monday reported a consolidated net loss of Rs 82 crore for the fourth quarter ended March 2020. It posted a net profit of Rs 48 crore in the January-March quarter a year ago. It posted a loss because the film exhibition business was impacted because of COVID-19 pandemic and subsequent lockdown. The company’s revenue from operations declined 22% to Rs 372 crore in FY20 against Rs 479 crore posted in Q4FY19. EBITDA in Q4FY20 grew 14% to Rs 110 crore against Rs 97 crore posted in Q4FY19. The margin grew to 29.6% against 20.1%.
State Bank of India (SBI): India’s largest lender SBI on Monday lowered its key lending rates – the marginal cost of funds-based lending rate (MCLR) and the external benchmark rate (EBR) - by 25 basis points (bps) and 40 bps respectively. Following the cut, effective 10 June, the one-year rate comes down to 7% and is the bank’s thirteenth consecutive MCLR reduction. It has also passed 40 bps cut in the repo rate to its borrowers who availed loans to EBR linked lending rate as well as Repo Linked Lending rate (RLLR). With this SBI’s EBR and RLLR came down by 40 bps to 6.65% and 6.25% respectively.
Sugar stocks: The government would be releasing assistance to the tune of Rs 1,100 crore on account of export & buffer subsidy to sugar mills. This would facilitate sugar mills in the clearance of their balance arrears.
UPL Ltd: UPL Corporation Limited, Mauritius, subsidiary of the agrochemicals major UPL, aims at raising about $500 million ($50 crore) via dollar bond issuance. It will use the proceeds to buy back bonds maturing in October 2021.
