NEW DELHI: Shares of Dhampur Sugar Mills traded 4.96 per cent up in Tuesday's trade at 11:27 am. Around 10,717 shares changed hands on the counter. The stock opened at Rs 95.25 and touched an intraday high and low of Rs 95.25 and Rs 95.25, respectively, in the session so far.
The company started production of hand sanitisers at Asmoli unit, Sambhal.
The stock quoted a 52-week high of Rs 252.75 and a 52-week low of Rs 67.85.
Dhampur Sugar Mills, incorporated in the year 1933, is a Small Cap company (having a market cap of Rs 605.12 crore) operating in Sugar sector.
The company reported net profit after tax of Rs 51.03 crore for the quarter ended 31 December, 2019. For the same quarter, the company has reported Consolidated sales of Rs 801.04 crore, up 15.43 per cent from last quarter Sales of Rs 693.95 crore and up 0.32 per cent from last year same quarter Sales of Rs 798.51 crore.
Dhampur Sugar Mills key products/revenue segments include chemicals which contributed Rs 486.66 crore to sales value (17.66 per cent of total sales), power which contributed Rs 224.59 crore to sales value (8.15 per cent of total sales), other operating revenue which contributed Rs 36.14 crore to sales value (1.31 per cent of total sales), others which contributed rs 30.87 crore to sales value (1.12 per cent of total sales), scrap which contributed Rs 5.82 crore to sales value (0.21 per cent of total sales) and duty drawback which contributed Rs 1.94 crore to sales value (0.07 per cent of total sales)for the year ending 31-mar-2019.
It quoted a price-to-earnings ratio of 2.87 and price to book value of 0.52.
