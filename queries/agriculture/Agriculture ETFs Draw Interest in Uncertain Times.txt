Exchange-traded funds focused on agricultural commodities have had a tough few years. However, the fact that their returns are largely uncorrelated to stocks and bonds could make them more attractive, especially if the economic outlook darkens.
There is a range of ETFs and exchange-traded notes (ETNs) that track the performance of commodities such as corn, wheat or soybeans. These funds usually trade in futures contracts, rather than holding the commodities directly.
