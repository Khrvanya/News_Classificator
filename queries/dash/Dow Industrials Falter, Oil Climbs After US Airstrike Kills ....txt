Qassem Soleimani, Iran’s powerful military commander, was killed in Iraq by a U.S. airstrike ordered by President Trump. WSJ’s Sune Rasmussen explains how his death may inflame tensions in the Middle East. Photo: Iraqi Prime Minister Press Office/Associated Press
Global stocks faltered and oil prices advanced after a U.S. airstrike in Baghdad killed an Iranian military leader, recharging tensions in the oil-rich Middle East.
The news put a brake on a recent winning stretch for U.S. stocks, with all three major indexes falling from record levels, and spurred a dash for haven assets like gold and government bonds. Brent crude gained 3.5% to $68.60, capping off a fifth straight week of gains and settling at the highest level since September.
