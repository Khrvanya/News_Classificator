Nasdaq, S&P 500 also rise despite coronavirus infections moving higher in southern U.S. states
U.S. stocks climbed Monday as investors weighed the rise in coronavirus infections and the likelihood of future lockdowns against hopes for more stimulus from central banks.
The Dow Jones Industrial Average gained 580.25 points, or 2.3%, to close at 25595.80, its biggest one-day increase in more than three weeks. The rebound came after the blue-chip index lost 3.3% last week.
