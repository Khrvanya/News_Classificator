Dow futures retreated Thursday evening as the U.S. House of Representatives passed a much-awaited $484 billion coronavirus aid package that includes a second round of funding for small businesses under the Paycheck Protection Plan, or PPP. Futures for the Dow Jones Industrial Average YMM20 slipped 51 points, or 0.2%, at 23,285, those for the S&P 500 index ESM20 were off 10.30 points, or 0.4%, at 2,779.50, while Nasdaq-100 futures NQM20 retreated 51 points, or 0.6%, at 8,547.75. The measure, which comes after extensive negotiations between Democrats and Republicans, contains about $320 billion for a PPP meant to help small businesses. It also has about $75 billion for hospitals, $25 billion for coronavirus testing and $60 billion for the Small Business Administration's Economic Injury Disaster Loan program.
