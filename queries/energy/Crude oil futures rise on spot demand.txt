New Delhi: Crude oil prices on Monday rose Rs 29 to Rs 3,019 per barrel as participants widened their positions on firm spot demand. On the Multi Commodity Exchange , crude oil for delivery in June traded higher by Rs 29, or 0.97 per cent, at Rs 3,019 per barrel in 5,919 lots.
Crude oil for July delivery was up by Rs 30, or 0.99 per cent, at Rs 3,055 per barrel with an open interest of 271 lots.
Analysts said raising of bets by participants kept crude prices higher in futures trade here.
Globally, West Texas Intermediate crude oil rose 1.11 per cent to USD 39.99 per barrel, while Brent Crude gained 1.35 per cent at USD 42.87 per barrel.
