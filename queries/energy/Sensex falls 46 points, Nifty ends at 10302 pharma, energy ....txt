New Delhi: The BSE Sensex and Nifty gave up all theirÂ intraday gains in the last hour of trade and ended lower on Tuesday as index heavyweights like Reliance Industries, TCS, HDFC Bank, ONGC witnessed selling pressure. Lower European shares and fall in US stock futures also impacted sentiment. The Sensex ended 45.72 points lower at 34,915.80. The index made a high of 35,233.91 and a low of 34,812.80 during intraday trade today. The Nifty meanwhile closed 10.30 points or 0.10% lower at 10,302.10. Pharma and energy stocks led losses in the market today.
Among Nifty stocks, BPCL was the top loser, down 2.44% followed by IOC and PowerGrid Corp, which fell 2.01% and 1.91% respectively. Sun Pharma, UPL, Coal India, Vedanta, Gail, Bharti Airtel, ONGC, Tata Motors, Bajaj Auto, RIL, IndusInd Bank were the other prominent losers in the Nifty.
Meanwhile, Shree Cements was the top gainer in Nifty, up 3.17% followed by Maruti Suzuki and Nestle, which gained 2.76% and 2.62% respectively. ICICI Bank, Britannia, Ultratech Cement, Tata Steel, Hero MotoCorp were the other big gainers in the Nifty.
Market breadth was negative. Out of the 1,630 stocks traded on NSE, 668 shares closed in the green while 962 shares ended with losses.
The rupee on Tuesday settled 7 paise higher at 75.51 against the US dollar. The rupee opened strong at 75.48 and settled for the day at 75.51 against the US dollar, up 7 paise over its previous close. It had settled at 75.58 against the greenback on Monday.
European shares traded mixed. Germany's DAX index was up 0.38%, London's FTSE 100 index fell 0.37% while France's CAC40 added 0.20%. US stock futures were also down up to 0.20%. Asian shares mostly closed higher. Japan's Nikkei rose 1.33%, Shanghai Composite added 0.78%, Taiwan Weighted settled 0.68% higher while Hang Seng advanced 0.52%.
