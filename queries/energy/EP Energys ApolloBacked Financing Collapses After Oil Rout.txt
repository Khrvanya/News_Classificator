Apollo Global Management Inc. and other firms are backing off a commitment to finance shale driller EP Energy Corp.’s exit from chapter 11, people familiar with the matter said, the second major bankruptcy deal to falter over the turmoil in U.S. energy markets.
The investors, which own large chunks of EP Energy’s debt, went back on a $475 million commitment to purchase its equity under a court-approved chapter 11 strategy, these people said. Without the financing commitment, EP Energy lacks a clear path out of chapter 11...
