On Monday, the Dow climbed 580 points. There was a 14.4% gain in Boeing’s stock due to the fact that certification flights for the Boeing 737 Max began Yesterday. The S&P also registered a gain, climbing 1.5%. Monday’s gains have brought the index into positive territory for the volatile month of June. The Nasdaq rose 1.2%, pushed by gains in Netflix, Microsoft, Facebook and Apple. Forward looking stock futures rose slightly in overnight trading pointing to gains at the open on Tuesday. The Dow futures rose 60 points, or 0.4%. The S&P 500 and Nasdaq-100 were also higher with gains of 0.4% and 0.45%.
The Stoxx 600 provisionally closed higher by around 0.6%. It was a consensus move for the EU pack with most sectors and major bourses in positive territory. Bank stocks were the top performers, climbing more than 2%. In a somewhat tad bit alarming piece of news British oil giant BP announced that it will sell its petrochemicals business to privately owned Ineos for $5 Bn. This is indicative of stress levels in the oil sector as prices and demand both seem volatile. Wirecard shares surged 154% on the back of news that they will resume operations despite solvency issues. However Wirecard shares are still quoting at penny value of just above 3 euros per share.
Stocks in Asia Pacific rose in Tuesday morning trade. Investors await China’s official manufacturing Purchasing Manager’s Index data which should give a better indication of how the recovery is panning out in the manufacturing sector. The Nikkei 225 in Japan rose 1.75% in early trade, following its more than 2% fall on Monday. The Topix index also added 1.5%. S Korean Kospi gained 1.39%, and the S&P/ASX 200 in Australia added 0.66%. The MSCI Asia ex-Japan index traded 0.33% higher
Oil prices rose about $1 a barrel on Monday. Brent crude rose 74 cents, or 1.8%, to $41.76 a barrel leading the charge. Brent is now set to end June with a third consecutive monthly gain. US WTI crude gained $1.21, or 3.1%, to $39.70 a barrel. News that the second wave finds a much better prepared US medical force coupled with reluctance to shut down yet again alleviated demand concerns for now.
