Mumbai: Indian equity markets are likely to open on a flat note on Friday (June 19) tracking mixed global cues. Nifty futures traded on Singapore Exchange (SGX Nifty) was up 3 points at 10,048 as of 7:10 am. Here are the other global cues you need to track before going into trade today.
Thursday's trading session was a bit volatile due to disappointing jobs data coupled with news of a second wave of coronavirus cases. U.S. Texas, California, Arizona and Florida all reported its biggest-ever one-day increase. Initial U.S. jobless claims totaled 1.5 million for last week, topping a Dow Jones estimate of 1.3 million. The Dow closed just below the flatline while the S&P 500 and Nasdaq managed small gains till close. However, US futures were little changed as Wall Street will try to finish the week in positive territory. Dow futures gained just 27 points pointing to a Friday opening dip of about 20 points. S&P 500 and Nasdaq-100 futures pointed to a flat Friday open.
Shares in Asia Pacific were mixed on Friday morning. Investor focus on Friday remains on developments surrounding the resurgence of coronavirus. Four states in the US are reporting record spikes in cases. Meanwhile, a Chinese health expert said that a recent virus outbreak in Beijing is under control. The Chinese capital saw a jump in infections after more than 50 days without domestically transmitted Covid-19 cases. Japan’s Nikkei 225 gained 0.18% while the Topix index shed 0.39%. Over in South Korea, the Kospi fell 0.75%. Shares in Australia saw gains, with the S&P/ASX 200 up 0.39%. Overall, the MSCI Asia ex-Japan index traded 0.14% lower.
EU (EU stocks off session lows; Bank of England expands bond buying and keeps interest rates 1%; Wirecard down 60%)
European stocks were lower on Thursday afternoon. The Bank of England expanded its bond-buying program and held interest rates at 0.1%. The Stoxx 600 finished off session lows but fell 0.6% amid a choppy session. Banks and basic resources dropped around 1.2% to lead losses while chemicals stocks bucked the downward trend to add 0.7%. German payments co Wirecard fell 60% after again delaying the publication of its 2019 financial report.
Oil prices rose on Thursday as a panel of OPEC and allies met to review supply cuts. The market remained concerned about additional coronavirus cases reported in parts of the United States and China. Brent crude futures were up 78 cents at $41.48 a barrel and WTI crude futures gained 88 cents, or 2.32%, to settle at $38.84 per barrel
GOLD (Gold slides as jobless claims fall, Beijing claims outbreak contained)
Gold fell on Thursday after data showed lower jobless claims in the United States and Beijing was bringing its latest coronavirus outbreak under control, but mounting infections globally limited losses for the safe-haven metal.
