Japan stocks higher at close of trade; Nikkei 225 up 0.11%
Investing.com – Japan stocks were higher after the close on Thursday, as gains in the Paper & Pulp , Railway & Bus and Real Estate sectors led shares higher.
At the close in Tokyo, the Nikkei 225 gained 0.11%.
The best performers of the session on the Nikkei 225 were Yamato Holdings Co., Ltd. (T: 9064 ), which rose 3.86% or 91.0 points to trade at 2446.0 at the close. Meanwhile, Sekisui House, Ltd. (T: 1928 ) added 3.45% or 69.0 points to end at 2067.0 and ANA Holdings Inc (T: 9202 ) was up 2.93% or 70.5 points to 2480.0 in late trade.
The worst performers of the session were Toho Zinc Co., Ltd. (T: 5707 ), which fell 3.41% or 52.0 points to trade at 1473.0 at the close. SUMCO Corp. (T: 3436 ) declined 3.08% or 50.0 points to end at 1576.0 and Unitika, Ltd. (T: 3103 ) was down 2.92% or 10.0 points to 332.0.
Falling stocks outnumbered advancing ones on the Tokyo Stock Exchange by 2482 to 1061 and 144 ended unchanged.
The Nikkei Volatility , which measures the implied volatility of Nikkei 225 options, was unchanged 0% to 28.12.
Crude oil for August delivery was up 0.85% or 0.34 to $40.16 a barrel. Elsewhere in commodities trading, Brent oil for delivery in September rose 0.83% or 0.35 to hit $42.38 a barrel, while the August Gold Futures contract fell 0.05% or 0.85 to trade at $1779.05 a troy ounce.
USD/JPY was up 0.03% to 107.50, while EUR/JPY rose 0.34% to 121.30.
The US Dollar Index Futures was down 0.23% at 96.935.
