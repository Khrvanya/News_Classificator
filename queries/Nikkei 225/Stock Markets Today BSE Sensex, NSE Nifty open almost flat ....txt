The stock markets on Thursday opened almost flat from Wednesday's close. While Sensex was trading at 33485.03, down by over 22 points or 0.07 per cent within minutes of its opening, the broader market, Nifty 50 traded at 9863, down by over 17 points or 0.2 per cent from the previous close.
Barring six stocks, all the stocks in the 30 share BSE Sensex were trading in the red. The top gainers on Thursday at the time of opening were Infosys, ITC, Power grid, Bharti Airtel and Nestle India. The top losers were ONGC, ICICI Bank, Kotak Mahindra Bank, Maruti Suzuki and Titan.
Meanwhile, Kotak Mahindra Bank, Britannia, Shree Cement and Adani Ports were the top gainers on Nifty Fifty on Thursday in the opening trade. The top losers were ONGC, JSW Steel, Hindalco Industries, Axis Bank and Indusind Bank.
On Wednesday, Sensex ended at 33507.92, down by 0.3 per cent while the Nifty 50 closed at 9881.15, also down by 0.3 per cent.
Today, the rupee was trading at around 76.2350 against the USD at 9:30 am.
Asian stocks were set to dip on Thursday after a choppy Wall Street session as spiking coronavirus cases and prospects of new lockdowns erased earlier confidence about a global economic recovery. Optimism about a quick economic comeback has been tempered by more global cases of the coronavirus, with an outbreak in Beijing and a rising infections in US states that are reopening their economies.
