Despite a lackluster year for growth, the common currency barely budged against the dollar in 2019
The euro had its calmest year ever in 2019, shrugging off its reputation as a volatile reflection of Europe’s perennially weak economy and never-ending political misfortunes. Many currency watchers seem to think the quiet can continue.
Despite a lackluster 2019 for European growth, government-bond yields plunging into negative territory and the drama of the U.K.’s exit from the European Union, the common currency had its narrowest yearly trading range against the dollar since its inception in 1999.
