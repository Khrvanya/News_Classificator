Natural gas-focused drillers rose on the notion that a wipeout for U.S. oil drillers could help them
Monday was about as dark a day as the U.S. energy industry has ever suffered. There was one bright spot, though.
The shale revolution has unlocked tremendous quantities of oil, improbably making the once-vulnerable U.S. a net exporter of petroleum products. But it also is producing so much natural gas that, even with a fleet of new export terminals, the country has been flaring off enough of the fuel to power a small country. Prices recently hit multidecade lows.
