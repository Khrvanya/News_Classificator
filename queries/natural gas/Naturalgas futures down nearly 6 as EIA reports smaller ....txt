The U.S. Energy Information Administration reported Thursday that domestic supplies of natural gas fell by 143 billion cubic feet for the week ended Feb. 21. Analysts polled by S&P Global Platts forecast a decline of 158 billion cubic feet. Total stocks now stand at 2.2 trillion cubic feet, up 637 billion cubic feet from a year ago, and 179 billion cubic feet above the five-year average, the government said. April natural gas ngj20 was down 11.2 cents, or 6.1%, at $1.725 per million British thermal units. It was trading at $1.749 shortly before the data .
