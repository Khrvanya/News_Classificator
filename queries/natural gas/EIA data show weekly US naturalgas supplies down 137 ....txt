The U.S. Energy Information Administration reported Thursday that domestic supplies of natural gas fell by 137 billion cubic feet for the week ended Jan. 31. That compared with a decline of 126 billion cubic feet forecast by analysts polled by S&P Global Platts. Total stocks now stand at 2.609 trillion cubic feet, up 615 billion cubic feet from a year ago, and 199 billion cubic feet above the five-year average, the government said. March natural gas ngh20 was flat at $1.861 per million British thermal units. It was trading modestly lower at $1.856 shortly before the data .
