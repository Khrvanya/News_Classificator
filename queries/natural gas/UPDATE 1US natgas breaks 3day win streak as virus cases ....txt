    July 1 (Reuters) - U.S. natural gas futures on Wednesday snapped a three-session gaining streak as
concerns about another lockdown due to surging coronavirus infections clouded demand outlook.
    The August gas futures contract fell 8 cents, or 4.6%, to settle at $1.671 per million
British thermal units, having hit its highest since June 12 in the last session.
    The front-month contract also posted its biggest quarterly rise since June 2018 on Tuesday.
