The U.S. Energy Information Administration reported Thursday that domestic supplies of natural gas fell by 19 billion cubic feet for the week ended March 27. Analysts, on average, expected a decline of 25 billion cubic feet, according to a poll conducted by S&P Global Platts. Total stocks now stand at 1.986 trillion cubic feet, up 863 billion cubic feet from a year ago, and 292 billion cubic feet above the five-year average, the government said. May natural gas ngk20 was down 4.4 cents, or 2.8%, at $1.543 per million British thermal units, little changed from before the data .
