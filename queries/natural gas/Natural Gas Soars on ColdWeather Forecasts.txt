Cold-weather forecasts across much of the U.S. are extending a rally in natural-gas prices, the latest example of shifting temperatures wrong-footing investors in the volatile commodity market.
Natural-gas futures rose 3.9% to $2.821 a million British thermal units on the New York Mercantile Exchange Monday, bringing their rally from a multiyear low hit three months ago to about 36%. Prices are at their highest level since late March.
