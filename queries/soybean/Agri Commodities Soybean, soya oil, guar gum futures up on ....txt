New Delhi: Soybean prices on Friday rose Rs 24 to Rs 3,696 per quintal in futures trade as speculators raised their positions, supported by a strong spot demand.
Marketmen said fresh positions created by participants following upsurge in domestic demand pushed up soybean prices.
On the National Commodity and Derivatives Exchange, soybean contracts for August delivery traded higher by Rs 24, or 0.65 per cent, to Rs 3,696 per quintal with a business turnover of 42,710 lots.
New Delhi, Aug 23 (PTI) Refined soya oil prices dipped 0.03 per cent to Rs 758.25 per 10 kg in futures trade on Friday as speculators trimmed their bets on adequate supply at the spot market.
On the National Commodity and Derivatives Exchange, refined soya oil for October contracts fell by 2 paise, or 0.03 per cent, to Rs 758.25 per 10 kg in 34,260 lots.
Market analysts said cutting down of positions by participants against ample stocks mainly influenced the prices.
Cottonseed oil cake prices rose by Rs 19.5 to Rs 3,112.5 per quintal in futures trade on Friday tracking an upbeat trend at physical market on higher demand.
On the National Commodity and Derivatives Exchange, cottonseed oil cake contracts for August delivery were trading up by Rs 19.5, or 0.63 per cent, to Rs 3,112.5 per quintal with an open interest of 32,160 lots.
Similarly, September contracts also traded up by Rs 11.5, or 0.55 per cent, to Rs 2,113 per quintal with an open of 31,360 lots.
Guar gum prices rose by Rs 59 to Rs 8,375 per five quintal in futures trade on Friday amid strong export demand at the spot market.
On the National Commodity and Derivatives Exchange, guar gum contracts for September delivery traded higher by Rs 59, or 0.71 per cent, at Rs 8,375 per five quintal with an open interest of 48,785 lots.
Guar gum for October delivery also rose by Rs 47, or 0.56 per cent, to Rs 8,481 per five quintal, mirroring an open interest of 19,460 lots.
Mustard seed prices rose by Rs 5 to Rs 4,002 per quintal in futures trade on Friday on accumulation of positions by trades at spot demand.
Marketmen attributed the uptick in mustard seed prices to pick-up in demand from oil mills amid tight arrivals from producing belts in physical markets.
On the National Commodity and Derivatives Exchange, mustard seed contracts for October delivery rose by Rs 5, or 0.13 per cent, to Rs 4,002 per quintal with an open interest of 34,250 lots.
Guar seed prices rose by Rs 10.5 to Rs 4,287 per 10 quintal in futures trade on Wednesday as participants widened their holdings, driven by a firm trend at the spot market.
On the National Commodity and Derivatives Exchange, guar seed contracts for September delivery increased by Rs 10.5, or 0.25 per cent, to Rs 4,287 per 10 quintal with an open interest of 85,210 lots.
Marketmen said raising of bets by speculators on a firm trend in spot market due to thin supplies from growing belts mainly led to the rise in prices here.
