Soybean prices on Wednesday rose by Rs 20 to Rs 3,742 per quintal in futures market as traders created fresh positions tracking positive spot demand.
On the National Commodity and Derivatives Exchange, soybean for March delivery rose by Rs 20, or 0.54 per cent, to Rs 3,742 per quintal with an open interest of 86,135 lots.
Soybean for April delivery went up by Rs 26 to Rs 3,720 per quintal with an open interest of 1,11,940 lots.
Market players said raising of fresh positions by speculators on the back of higher demand mainly led to rise in soybean prices.
Refined soya oil prices on Wednesday rose 1.08 per cent to Rs 788.4 per 10 kg in futures trade as speculators raised their bets driven by a firm trend in spot market.
On the National Commodity and Derivatives Exchange, refined soya oil for March delivery moved up by Rs 8.4, or 1.08 per cent, to Rs 788.4 per 10 kg in 26,645 lots.
Similarly, refined soya oil for April delivery edged up by Rs 6.6, or 0.86 per cent, to Rs 777 per 10 kg in 29,300 lots.
Analysts said widening of positions by traders mainly helped refined soya oil prices to trade higher in futures market here.
Guar seed prices on Wednesday rose by Rs 20 to Rs 3,774 per 10 quintal in futures trade after speculators increased their positions driven by a firm trend at spot market.
On the National Commodity and Derivatives Exchange, guar seed for March contracts increased by Rs 20, or 0.53 per cent, to Rs 3,774 per 10 quintal with an open interest of 37,600 lots.
Similarly, guar seed for April delivery gained Rs 12, or 0.32 per cent, to Rs 3,772 per 10 quintal with an open interest of 58,095 lots.
According to marketmen, raising of bets by speculators tracking a firm trend in spot market on thin supplies from growing belts mainly led to rise in guar seed prices here.
Mustard seed prices on Wednesday rose by Rs 23 to Rs 3,999 per quintal in futures trade as participants widened their bets amid firm spot demand.
Marketmen said apart from restricted arrivals from growing belts, demand from oil mills in spot market mainly lifted mustard seed prices here.
On the National Commodity and Derivatives Exchange, mustard seed for delivery in April rose by Rs 23, or 0.58 per cent, to Rs 3,999 per quintal with an open interest of 17,570 lots.
Similarly, the delivery for May gained Rs 23, or 0.57 per cent, to Rs 4,024 per quintal with an open interest of 5,080 lots. \
Guar gum prices on Wednesday climbed Rs 96 to Rs 6,249 per five quintal in futures trade as speculators widened their holdings driven by firm trend at spot market.
On the National Commodity and Derivatives Exchange, guar gum for March delivery traded higher 1.5 per cent, or Rs 96, to Rs 6,249 per five quintal with an open interest of 14,665 lots.
Likewise, the contracts for April delivery was trading up by Rs 91, or 1.46 per cent, to Rs 6,325 per five quintal as open interest stood at 44,155 lots.
Analysts said after tracking firm physical market trend, traders raised their bets which led to rise in guar gum prices.
Cottonseed oil cake prices on Wednesday traded up by Rs 59 to Rs 1,731 per quintal in futures trade as speculators created fresh positions driven by firm spot demand.
On the National Commodity and Derivatives Exchange, cottonseed oil cake for delivery in March traded higher by Rs 59, or 3.53 per cent, to Rs 1,731 per quintal with an open interest of 62,850 lots.
Likewise for delivery in April cottonseed oil cake traded up by Rs 58, or 3.43 per cent, to Rs 1,751 per quintal with an open interest of 51,930 lots.
Marketmen said raising of positions by participants amid a rising demand from cattle-feed makers mainly influenced cottonseed oil cake prices here.
Coriander prices on Wednesday rose by Rs 24 to Rs 5,905 per quintal in futures trade on firm demand in spot market.
On the National Commodity and Derivatives Exchange, coriander for April delivery shot up by Rs 24, or 0.41 per cent, to Rs 5,905 per quintal with an open interest of 7,050 lots.
Similarly, the spice for May delivery traded higher by Rs 44, or 0.73 per cent, at Rs 5,906 per quintal in 1,410 lots.
A firm trend in spot market and restricted supplies from producing regions pushed coriander prices up, market analysts said.
