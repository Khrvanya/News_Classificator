New Delhi: Soybean prices on Wednesday increased by Rs 32 to Rs 3,850 per quintal in futures trade as speculators raised their positions supported by a strong spot demand.
Marketmen said fresh positions created by participants following upsurge in domestic demand pushed up soybean prices here.
On the National Commodity and Derivatives Exchange, soybean contracts for September delivery traded higher by Rs 32, or 0.84 per cent, to Rs 3,850 per quintal in an open interest of 28,300 lots.
Refined soya oil prices rose 0.24 per cent to Rs 760.6 per 10 kg in futures trade on Wednesday as speculators created fresh positions.
On the National Commodity and Derivatives Exchange, refined soya oil contracts for September delivery went up by Rs 1.8, or 0.24 per cent, to Rs 760.6 per 10 kg with an open interest of 36,390 lots.
Similarly, the contracts for October delivery traded higher by Rs 1.25, or 0.16 per cent, at Rs 759.7 per 10 kg with an open interest of 22,680 lots.
Analysts said fresh positions built up by traders against restricted arrivals from growing regions mainly led to rise in refined soya oil prices.
Coriander prices rose 1.56 per cent to Rs 5,985 per quintal in futures market on Wednesday as participants built up fresh positions amid rising spot demand.
Besides, tight stocks on account of restricted arrivals from major growing regions pushed up coriander prices.
On the National Commodity and Derivatives Exchange (NCDEX), coriander contracts for most-traded September delivery edged higher by Rs 92, or 1.56 per cent, to Rs 5,985 per quintal with an open interest of 25,330 lots.
Similarly, coriander contracts for October delivery traded higher by Rs 78, or 1.29 per cent, at Rs 6,110 per quintal, with an open interest of 10,390 lots.
Market analysts attributed the rise in coriander futures to upsurge in demand at physical market against tight stocks position.
Mustard seed prices were up 0.46 per cent to Rs 3,947 per quintal in the futures trade on Wednesday as participants widened their holdings tracking a firm trend at the spot market.
On the National Commodity and Derivatives Exchange (NCDEX), mustard seed contracts for the September delivery rose by Rs 18, or 0.46 per cent, to Rs 3,947 per quintal with an open interest of 46,140 lots.
Mustard seed contracts for October delivery also traded higher by Rs 43, or 1.08 per cent, at Rs 4,032 per quintal in 50,990 lots.
Marketmen said strong demand from oil millers mainly pushed up mustard seed prices here.
Cottonseed oil cake prices dipped by Rs 29 to Rs 3,218 per quintal in futures trade on Wednesday after tracking a muted trend at the spot market.
On the National Commodity and Derivatives Exchange, cottonseed oil cake to be delivered in September contracts were down by Rs 29, or 0.89 per cent, to Rs 3,218 per quintal with an open interest of 30,880 lots.
Marketmen said the slide in cottonseed oil cake prices was mainly due to tightening of bets by participants triggered by lack of demand from animal feed makers.
Guar gum prices rose by Rs 24 to Rs 8,300 per five quintal in futures trade on Wednesday amid strong export demand at the spot market.
On the National Commodity and Derivatives Exchange, guar gum contracts for September delivery traded higher by Rs 24, or 0.29 per cent, at Rs 8,300 per five quintal with an open interest of 37,720 lots.
Guar gum for October delivery also rose by Rs 14, or 0.17 per cent, to Rs 8,400 per five quintal mirroring an open interest of 30,470 lots.
Guar seed prices rose by Rs 9 to Rs 4,309 per 10 quintal in futures trade on Wednesday as participants widened their holdings driven by a firm trend at the spot market.
On the National Commodity and Derivatives Exchange, guar seed contracts for September delivery increased by Rs 9, or 0.21 per cent, to Rs 4,309 per 10 quintal with an open interest of 74,040 lots.
Marketmen said raising of bets by speculators on a firm trend in spot market due to thin supplies from growing belts mainly led to rise in prices here.
