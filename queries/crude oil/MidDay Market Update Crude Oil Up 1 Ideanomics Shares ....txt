Midway through trading Monday, the Dow traded up 0.43% to 25983.65 while the NASDAQ rose 0.77% to 10023.06. The S&P also rose, gaining 0.49% to 3112.82.
The U.S. has the highest number of coronavirus cases and deaths in the world, reporting a total of 2,280,960 cases with around 119,970 deaths. Brazil confirmed a total of over 1,083,340 COVID-19 cases with 50,590 deaths, while Russia reported a total of at least 591,460 confirmed cases and 8,190 deaths. In total, there were at least 8,970,970 cases of COVID-19 worldwide with over 468,580 deaths, according to data compiled by Johns Hopkins University.
Utilities shares climbed 1.8% on Monday. Meanwhile, top gainers in the sector included Centrais Eletricas Brasileiras S.A. - Eletrobras (NYSE: EBR ), up 6%, and Clearway Energy Inc (NYSE: CWEN ), up 5%.
American Airlines Group Inc (NASDAQ: AAL ) plans to sell shares and convertible senior notes due 2025 to raise $3.5 billion to ease the liquidity crisis during the pandemic.
On Sunday, American Airlines proposed a public offering of $750 million of its common stock as well as $750 million worth of convertible senior notes due 2025.
Evoke Pharma, Inc. (NASDAQ: EVOK ) shares shot up 76% to $4.125 following FDA approval of GIMOTI. HC Wainwright & Co. also upgraded the company's stock from Neutral to Buy and announced a $10 price target.
Shares of Ideanomics, Inc. (NASDAQ: IDEX ) got a boost, shooting 77% to $3.93 after the company received a 200 electric vehicle order worth $3.2 million.
Francesca's Holdings Corporation (NASDAQ: FRAN ) shares were also up, gaining 33% to $4.8999.
Urban One, Inc. (NASDAQ: UONE ) shares tumbled 40% to $21.83 after gaining 45% on Friday.
Shares of Carver Bancorp, Inc. (NASDAQ: CARV ) were down 26% to $9.02.
Nabriva Therapeutics plc (NASDAQ: NBRV ) was down, falling 8% to $0.7561 after the company received a Complete Response Letter from the FDA on NDA for CONTEPO (fosfomycin) for injection for the treatment of complicated urinary tract infections.
In commodity news, oil traded up 1% to $40.15, while gold traded up 1.1% to $1,772.30.
Silver traded up 0.8% Monday to $17.985, while copper rose 1.4% to $2.647.
European shares were lower today. The eurozone’s STOXX 600 fell 0.74%, the Spanish Ibex Index fell 0.92%, while Italy’s FTSE MIB Index dropped 0.71%. Meanwhile, the German DAX 30 declined 0.55%, French CAC 40 fell 0.62% and UK shares slipped 0.76%.
The Chicago Fed National Activity Index climbed to 2.61 in May, versus a revised reading of -17.89 for April.
Sales of existing houses in the US fell 9.7% to an annual rate of 3.91 million units in May.
The Treasury is set to auction 3-and 6-month bills at 11:30 a.m. ET.
