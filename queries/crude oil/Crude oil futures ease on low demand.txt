New Delhi: Crude oil futures on Friday plummeted 3.07 per cent to Rs 1,513 per barrel as participants trimmed their positions on weak spot demand.
On the Multi Commodity Exchange, crude oil for April delivery fell by Rs 48, or 3.07 per cent, to Rs 1,513 per barrel with a business volume of 33,707 lots.
Crude oil for May delivery was quoting lower by Rs 53, or 2.52 per cent, to Rs 2,053 per barrel with an open interest of 7,766 lots.
Analysts said the fall in crude oil futures was mostly due to trimming of positions by participants amid low demand.
Globally, West Texas Intermediate crude oil fell 4.38 per cent to $19 per barrel, however, Brent Crude rose 1.01 per cent to $28.10 per barrel in New York.
