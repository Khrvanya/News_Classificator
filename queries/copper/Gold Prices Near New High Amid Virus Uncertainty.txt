Gold prices rose Monday, nearing a new 7½-year high with a recent rise in coronavirus cases lifting demand for the haven metal.
Front-month gold futures for delivery in June rose 0.6% to $1,756.70 a troy ounce on the Comex division of the New York Mercantile Exchange, closing just below their highest level since October 2012. Prices are up 16% for the year, boosted by coronavirus-related economic uncertainty and expectations for ultralow interest rates.
