Nickel prices on Thursday edged up by Rs 11 to Rs 1,040.30 per kg in futures trade as speculators built positions owing to rising demand from alloy-makers in domestic market.
On the Multi Commodity Exchange, nickel contracts for January delivery gained Rs 11, or 1.07 per cent, to Rs 1,040.30 per kg in a business turnover of 4,651 lots.
The metal for delivery in February gained Rs 11.70, or 1.14 per cent, at Rs 1,038.90 per kg in a business turnover of 68 lots.
Rising demand in spot market from alloy-makers mainly supported price rise in nickel futures, analysts said.
Copper prices on Thursday traded up by 0.67 per cent at Rs 443.75 per kg in futures market on the back of pick-up in spot demand.
On the Multi Commodity Exchange, copper contracts for delivery in January traded higher by Rs 2.95, or 0.67 per cent, at Rs 443.75 per kg in a business turnover of 2,366 lots.
Similarly, the metal for delivery in February edged up by Rs 2.80, or 0.63 per cent, to Rs 447.55 per kg in 78 lots.
Analysts attributed the rise in copper prices to raising of bets by participants driven by a pick-up in demand in spot market.
