New Delhi: Nickel prices on Thursday fell 1.07 per cent to Rs 923 per kg in futures trade after participants trimmed their positions amid weak demand in spot market.
On the Multi Commodity Exchange, nickel contracts for March delivery fell by Rs 10, or 1.07 per cent, to Rs 923 per kg with a business turnover of 3,702 lots.
Similarly, nickel contracts for April delivery dropped by Rs 10.10, or 1.08 per cent, to Rs 922 per kg in 65 lots.
Analysts said subdued demand in spot market mainly led to the fall in nickel prices here.
Copper futures on Thursday traded 1.41 per cent lower at Rs 419.50 per kg after participants offloaded their positions on weak spot demand.
On the Multi Commodity Exchange, copper contracts for March delivery fell by Rs 6, or 1.41 per cent, to Rs 419.50 per kg in a business turnover of 3,765 lots.
Similarly, copper contracts for April delivery traded lower by Rs 5.55, or 1.3 per cent, to Rs 422.75 per kg in a business turnover of 106 lots.
Traders said subdued demand in spot market mainly pushed down copper prices here.
Lead prices on Thursday drifted lower by 0.42 per cent to Rs 141.70 per kg in futures trade as speculators cut bets on low demand.
On the Multi Commodity Exchange, lead for delivery in May fell by 60 paise, or 0.42 per cent, to Rs 141.70 per kg in 30 lots.
Analysts said off-loading of positions by participants owing to slackened demand from consuming industries in the physical market mainly exerted pressure on lead prices here.
