Zinc prices on Thursday fell by 0.55 per cent to Rs 180.60 per kg in futures trade as speculators cut bets amid easing demand at spot market.
On the Multi Commodity Exchange, zinc contracts for May delivery moved down by Re 1, or 0.55 per cent, to Rs 180.60 per kg in a business turnover of 3 lots.
Analysts said offloading of positions by traders owing to slackened demand from consuming industries in physical market mainly weighed on zinc prices in futures trade here.
Nickel futures on Thursday declined by Rs 2.20 to Rs 934 per kg due to reducing of positions by speculators amid sluggish demand at spot market.
On the Multi Commodity Exchange, nickel contracts for February delivery fell by Rs 2.20, or 0.23 per cent, to Rs 934 per kg with a business turnover of 2,910 lots.
Similarly, nickel contracts for March delivery dropped by Rs 1.90, or 0.20 per cent, to Rs 932.40 per kg in 22 lots.
Analysts said subdued demand in spot market mainly led to fall in nickel future prices here.
Aluminium prices on Thursday fell by Rs 1.50 to Rs 140.50 per kg in futures market as participants trimmed their positions on low spot demand.
On the Multi Commodity Exchange, aluminium for delivery in May declined by Rs 1.50, or 1.06 per cent, to Rs 140.50 per kg in 2 lots.
Analysts said cutting down of positions by participants on easing demand from consuming industries in physical market mainly kept aluminium prices lower here.
Copper futures on Thursday traded 0.69 per cent lower at Rs 426.45 per kg as speculators reduced their exposure on weak spot demand.
On the Multi Commodity Exchange, copper contracts for February delivery fell by Rs 2.95, or 0.69 per cent, to Rs 426.45 per kg in a business turnover of 3,061 lots.
Similarly, copper contracts for March delivery traded lower by Rs 3.55, or 0.82 per cent, at Rs 428.30 per kg in a business turnover of 80 lots.
Traders said weak demand in spot market mainly pushed down copper prices here.
