Lead prices on Monday traded higher by 35 paise to Rs 149.40 per kg in futures trade as participants built up fresh positions driven by pick-up in spot demand.
On the Multi Commodity Exchange , lead for delivery in May edged up by 35 paise, or 0.23 per cent, to Rs 149.40 per kg with a business volume of 3 lots.
Market analysts said fresh positions created by traders due to upsurge in demand by battery-makers in physical market mainly kept lead prices higher.
Nickel prices on Monday edged up Rs 3.40 to Rs 955.50 per kg in futures trade as speculators built positions on rising demand from alloy-makers at spot market.
On the Multi Commodity Exchange, nickel contracts for February delivery gained Rs 3.40, or 0.36 per cent, to Rs 955.50 per kg in a business turnover of 2,071 lots.
Similarly, the metal for March delivery traded higher by Rs 4.90, or 0.52 per cent, at Rs 956.30 per kg in a business turnover of 59 lots.
Rising demand in spot market from alloy-makers mainly supported price rise in nickel futures, analysts said.
Copper prices on Monday traded up 0.47 per cent at Rs 436 per kg in futures market on the back of pick-up in spot demand.
On the Multi Commodity Exchange, copper contracts for delivery in February traded higher by Rs 2.05 or 0.47 per cent, at Rs 436 per kg in a business turnover of 2,546 lots.
Similarly, the metal for delivery in March edged up by Rs 2.15, or 0.49 per cent, to Rs 439 per kg in 139 lots.
Analysts attributed the rise in copper prices to raising of bets by participants driven by pick-up in spot demand.
