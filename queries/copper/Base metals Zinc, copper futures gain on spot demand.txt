New Delhi: Zinc prices on Wednesday rose 0.5 per cent to Rs 152 per kg in futures trade tracking a firm trend in physical markets on the back of pick-up in demand.
On the Multi Commodity Exchange, zinc contracts for May delivery traded higher by 75 paise, or 0.5 per cent, at Rs 152 per kg with a business turnover of 11,639 lots.
Marketmen said widening of positions by participants, following pick-up in demand from consuming industries, kept zinc prices higher.
Aluminium prices on Wednesday softened 0.04 per cent to Rs 131.50 per kg in futures trade as speculators cut bets amid subdued demand in spot market.
On the Multi Commodity Exchange, aluminium for delivery in May contracts eased by 5 paise, or 0.04 per cent, to Rs 131.50 per kg in a business turnover of 3,921 lots. However, the metal for delivery in June contracts was trading up by 25 paise, or 0.19 per cent, at Rs 132.75 per kg in 41 lots.
Analysts said off-loading of positions by participants owing to slackened demand from consuming industries in the physical market weighed on aluminium prices in futures trade.
Copper prices on Wednesday traded 0.36 per cent higher at Rs 403.75 per kg in futures market on the back of pick-up in spot demand.
On the Multi Commodity Exchange, copper contracts for delivery in May traded higher by Rs 1.45, or 0.36 per cent, at Rs 403.75 per kg in a business turnover of 3,275 lots.
Analysts attributed the rise in copper prices to raising of bets by participants driven by pick-up in spot demand.
Nickel prices on Wednesday rose 0.89 per cent to Rs 928.10 per kg in futures trade as speculators built positions on rising demand from alloy makers in spot market.
On the Multi Commodity Exchange, nickel contracts for May delivery gained Rs 8.20, or 0.89 per cent, to Rs 928.10 per kg in a business turnover of 708 lots.
Rising demand in spot market from alloy makers mainly supported price rise in nickel futures, analysts said.
