Nickel prices edged up by Rs 12.40 to Rs 1,206 per kg in futures trade on Thursday as speculators built their positions owing to rising demand from alloy-makers in the spot market.
On the Multi Commodity Exchange, nickel contracts for October delivery gained Rs 12.40, or 1.04 per cent, to Rs 1,206 per kg in a business turnover of 6,610 lots.
Similarly, the metal for delivery in November traded up by Rs 13.10, or 1.11 per cent, at Rs 1,198 per kg in a business turnover of 2,196 lots.
Rising demand at spot market from alloy-makers supported price rise in nickel futures, analysts said.
Zinc prices gained 30 paise at Rs 186 per kg in futures market on Thursday as traders built up fresh positions amid increase in spot demand.
On the Multi Commodity Exchange, zinc for delivery in October traded higher by 30 paise, or 0.16 per cent, to Rs 186 per kg in a business turnover of 2,147 lots.
Similarly, the metal for delivery in November edged up by 50 paise, or 0.27 per cent, at Rs 188.10 per kg in 571 lots.
Market analysts said fresh positions created by participants on rise in demand from consuming industries mainly attributed to gain in zinc prices.
Lead futures dropped 15 paise at Rs 155.95 per kg in futures trade on Thursday due to subdued demand at the physical market.
On the Multi Commodity Exchange, lead to be delivered in October traded lower by 15 paise, or 0.1 per cent, at Rs 155.95 per kg in a business turnover of 817 lots.
Similarly, lead contracts for November delivery declined by 5 paise, 0.03 per cent, to Rs 156.80 per kg in a business turnover of 240 lots.
Analysts said low demand from battery-makers in the spot market mainly led to fall in lead prices at futures trade here.
Copper prices traded up by 65 paise at Rs 440.25 per kg in futures trade on Thursday due to pick-up in spot demand.
Higher prices of the metal in global markets also influenced the sentiment, traders said.
On the Multi Commodity Exchange, copper contracts for October delivery traded higher by 65 paise, or 0.15 per cent, to Rs 440.25 per kg in 2,117 lots.
Similarly, the metal for delivery in November edged up by 15 paise, or 0.03 per cent, to Rs 445.25 per kg in 463 lots.
Aluminium prices fell 15 paise to Rs 131.10 per kg on Thursday as participants trimmed their positions amid a weak trend in spot market.
On the Multi Commodity Exchange, aluminium for delivery in October declined by 15 paise, or 0.11 per cent, to Rs 131.10 per kg in 398 lots.
Similarly, the metal for delivery in November weakened by 10 paise at Rs 133.35 per kg in 198 lots.
Analysts said cutting down of positions by participants on easing demand from consuming industries in physical market mainly kept aluminium prices lower here.
