Copper prices on Wednesday traded 0.35 per cent up at Rs 440.70 per kg in futures trade amid pick-up in spot demand.
Higher prices of the metal in the global market also influenced the sentiment, traders said.
On the Multi Commodity Exchange, copper contracts for December delivery traded higher by Rs 1.55, or 0.35 per cent, at Rs 440.70 per kg in a business turnover of 6,316 lots.
Similarly, the metal for delivery in January edged up by Rs 1.55, or 0.35 per cent, to Rs 445.05 per kg in 554 lots.
Zinc prices rose 0.22 per cent to Rs 182 per kg in futures market on Wednesday as traders built up fresh positions amid rise in spot demand.
On the Multi Commodity Exchange, zinc for delivery in December traded higher by 40 paise, or 0.22 per cent, to Rs 182 per kg in a business turnover of 3,596 lots.
Market analysts said fresh positions created by participants on the back of rise in demand from consuming industries mainly attributed to gain in zinc prices.
Nickel prices rose 0.19 per cent to Rs 993.40 per kg in futures market on Wednesday as traders built up fresh positions following higher spot demand.
At the Multi Commodity Exchange, Nickel for delivery in December traded higher by Re 1, or 0.19 per cent, to Rs 993.40 per kg in a business turnover of 2,041 lots.
Similarly, the metal for delivery in January edged up by Rs 1.70, or 0.17 per cent, to Rs 987.70 per kg in 199 lots.
Market analysts said fresh positions created by participants on the back of rise in demand from consuming industries.
Lead prices on Wednesday rose 40 paise to Rs 153.65 per kg in futures trade due to pick up in demand in spot market.
On the Multi Commodity Exchange, lead contracts for December delivery traded up by 40 paise, or 0.26 per cent, at Rs 153.65 per kg in a business turnover of 1,193 lots.
Market analysts attributed the rise in lead futures to pick up in demand from battery makers in spot market.
