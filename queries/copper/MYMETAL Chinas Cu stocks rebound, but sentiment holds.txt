Retail stocks of refined copper across China reversed up last week after decreasing for four straight weeks as downstream demand saw signs of weakness, according to Mymetal’s weekly report. However, the disruptions to production of overseas miners caused by COVID-19 are finally being reflected in copper concentrate supplies, which served to prop up both copper prices and sentiment.
Refined copper stocks in the warehouses of the Shanghai Futures Exchange (SHFE) and those in nine key cities for copper nationwide increased by a tiny 7,800 tonnes on week to reach 219,700 tonnes as of June 5, Mymetal’s survey showed.
