New Delhi: Zinc futures traded lower at Rs 192.30 per kg on Wednesday as speculators reduced exposure amid subdued demand at the spot market.
Zinc contracts for August delivery fell by 35 paise, or 0.18 per cent, to Rs 192.30 per kg at the Multi Commodity Exchange , clocking a business turnover of 3,181 lots.
On similar lines, zinc contracts to be delivered in September traded lower by 40 paise, or 0.21 per cent, to Rs 193.25 per kg in a business turnover of 99 lots.
Traders said the weakness in zinc prices at futures trade was mostly due to cutting down of bets by speculators in tandem with a weak trend at the spot market.
Nickel prices went up by 0.41 per cent to Rs 1,001.40 per kg in futures trade on Wednesday as speculators raised their bets on spot demand.
On the Multi Commodity Exchange, nickel contracts for August delivery gained Rs 4.10, or 0.41 per cent, to Rs 1,001.40 per kg in a business turnover of 11,396 lots.
The metal contracts for September delivery went up by Rs 4.20 to Rs 1,008.90 per kg in a business turnover of 417 lots.
Increase in domestic demand from alloy-makers and firmness in base metals overseas influenced nickel prices at futures trade here, analysts said.
Lead prices were trading lower at Rs 152.45 per kg in the futures trade on Wednesday after participants booked profits amid subdued demand at the spot market.
At the Multi Commodity Exchange, lead contracts for August delivery traded lower by 35 paise, or 0.23 per cent, at Rs 152.45 per kg in a business turnover of 1,913 lots.
Analysts attributed the fall in lead prices to profit-booking by players at current levels coupled with low demand at the spot market.
Besides, lead contracts for September delivery edged lower by 25 paise, or 0.16 per cent, to Rs 153.15 per kg in a business turnover of 27 lots.
Copper futures traded lower by 0.04 per cent at Rs 448.50 per kg on Wednesday as speculators offloaded their bets to book profits.
Besides, subdued demand at the domestic spot market further pulled down metal prices here.
On the Multi Commodity Exchange, copper contracts for the July delivery shed 20 paise, or 0.04 per cent, to Rs 448.50 per kg in a business turnover of 34 lots.
Similarly, the metal contracts for August delivery traded lower by 10 paise, or 0.02 per cent, to Rs 445.55 per kg in a business turnover of 5,643 lots.
Analysts attributed the fall in copper prices to offloading of positions by participants amid muted demand at the domestic spot market.
Aluminium prices on Wednesday edged up by 0.07 per cent to Rs 141.20 per kg in the futures market, after speculators built up fresh positions amid positive trend at the spot market.
On the Multi Commodity Exchange (MCX), aluminium for delivery in August contracts edged higher by 10 paise, or 0.07 per cent, to Rs 141.20 per kg in a business turnover of 2,320 lots.
Analysts said fresh positions created by traders after positive demand from consuming industries mainly led to a rise in aluminium prices.
