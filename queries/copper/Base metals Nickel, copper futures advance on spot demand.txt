New Delhi: Nickel prices on Monday rose 0.02 per cent to Rs 937.80 per kg in futures trade as speculators built positions on rising demand from alloy makers in spot market.
On the Multi Commodity Exchange, nickel contracts for May delivery gained 20 paise, or 0.02 per cent, to Rs 937.80 per kg in a business turnover of 1,458 lots.
Similarly, the metal for June delivery traded higher by Rs 1.30, or 0.14 per cent, at Rs 938.70 per kg in a business turnover of 203 lots.
Rising demand in spot market from alloy makers mainly supported the price rise in nickel futures, analysts said.
Copper prices on Monday traded 0.32 per cent higher at Rs 408.95 per kg in the futures market on the back of pick-up in spot demand.
On the Multi Commodity Exchange, copper contracts for delivery in May traded higher by Rs 1.30, or 0.32 per cent, at Rs 408.95 per kg in a business turnover of 3,629 lots.
Analysts attributed the rise in copper prices to raising of bets by participants driven by pick-up in spot demand.
Zinc prices on Monday rose 0.81 per cent to Rs 155.55 per kg in futures trade tracking a firm trend in physical markets on the back of increase in demand.
On the Multi Commodity Exchange, zinc futures contracts for May delivery traded higher by Rs 1.25, or 0.81 per cent, at Rs 155.55 per kg with a business turnover of 13,558 lots.
Marketmen said widening of positions by participants, following pick-up in demand from consuming industries, kept zinc prices higher.
