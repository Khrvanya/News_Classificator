Nickel prices on Wednesday edged up by Rs 11.50 to Rs 957.80 per kg in futures trade as speculators built positions on rising demand from alloy-makers at the spot market.
On the Multi Commodity Exchange, nickel contracts for February delivery gained Rs 11.50, or 1.22 per cent, to Rs 957.80 per kg in a business turnover of 3,530 lots.
Similarly, the metal for March delivery traded higher by Rs 10.90, or 1.15 per cent, at Rs 955 per kg in a business turnover of 40 lots.
Rising demand in spot market from alloy-makers mainly supported price rise in nickel futures , analysts said.
Copper prices on Wednesday traded up by Rs 4.50 at Rs 433.10 per kg in futures market as speculators widened their holdings on the back of spot demand.
On the Multi Commodity Exchange, copper contracts for delivery in February traded higher by Rs 4.50, or 1.05 per cent, at Rs 433.10 per kg in a business turnover of 3,550 lots.
Similarly, the metal for delivery in March edged up by Rs 3.95, or 0.92 per cent, to Rs 434.15 per kg in 81 lots.
Analysts attributed the rise in copper prices to raising of bets by participants driven by a pick-up in spot demand.
Zinc prices on Wednesday edged higher 0.49 per cent to Rs 174.15 per kg in futures market as traders built up fresh positions.
On the Multi Commodity Exchange, zinc for delivery in May traded higher by 85 paise, or 0.49 per cent, to Rs 174.15 per kg in a business turnover of 2 lots.
Market analysts said fresh positions created by participants on the back of rise in demand from consuming industries mainly attributed to gain in zinc prices.
