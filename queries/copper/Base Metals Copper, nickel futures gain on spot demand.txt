New Delhi: Copper prices on Friday traded up 0.29 per cent at Rs 375.90 per kg in futures market on the back of pick-up in spot demand.
On the Multi Commodity Exchange, copper contracts for delivery in March traded higher by Rs 1.10, or 0.29 per cent, at Rs 375.90 per kg in a business turnover of 4,141 lots.
Similarly, the metal for delivery in April edged up by Rs 1.05, or 0.28 per cent, to Rs 377.60 per kg in 413 lots.
Analysts attributed the rise in copper prices to raising of bets by participants driven by pick-up in spot demand.
Nickel prices on Friday gained 1.99 per cent to Rs 871 per kg in futures trade as speculators built positions on rising demand from alloy-makers at spot market.
On the Multi Commodity Exchange, nickel contracts for March delivery gained Rs 17, or 1.99 per cent, to Rs 871 per kg in a business turnover of 4,280 lots.
Similarly, the metal for April delivery traded higher by Rs 15.60, or 1.84 per cent, at Rs 864 per kg in a business turnover of 102 lots.
Rising demand in spot market from alloy-makers mainly supported price rise in nickel futures, analysts said.
