New Delhi: Copper futures on Monday fell 4.55 per cent to Rs 355.80 per kg as speculators reduced their exposure due to weak spot demand.
On the Multi Commodity Exchange, copper contracts for the March month delivery traded lower by Rs 16.95, or 4.55 per cent, to Rs 355.80 per kg in a business turnover of 2,549 lots.
Similarly, copper contracts for the April month delivery fell Rs 16.70, or 4.45 per cent, to Rs 358.30 per kg in a business turnover of 621 lots.
Traders attributed the subdued demand in spot market mainly to lower copper prices.
Nickel prices on Monday fell 2.93 per cent to Rs 835.50 per kg in futures trade after participants trimmed their positions amid weak demand at spot market.
On the Multi Commodity Exchange, nickel contracts for March delivery fell by Rs 25.20, or 2.93 per cent, to Rs 835.50 per kg with a business turnover of 2,270 lots.
Similarly, nickel contracts for April delivery slumped by Rs 25.70, or 3.01 per cent, to Rs 829.30 per kg in 209 lots.
Analysts said subdued demand in spot market mainly led to fall in nickel prices here.
