The Australian share market ended the week in the red, with the travel sector performing badly as COVID-19 pandemic fears returned to haunt investors.
The S&P/ASX 200 index closed on Friday with a 1.5 per cent gain at 5904.10 as banks performed strongly.
Commonwealth Bank rose 2.4 per cent to $69.27 and Westpac climbed 3.3 per cent to $18.40 while National Australia Bank rose 2.7 per cent to $18.40 and ANZ climbed 3 per cent to $18.80.
But the index ended the week 0.7 per cent lower. There were notable losses on Thursday, when the Australian market sank 2.5 per cent, joining Wall Street in a heavy sell-off as cases climbed sharply in United States.
Australia has its own second-wave concerns, with several parts of Melbourne designated as virus hotspots because of rising numbers of coronavirus infections.
Coles and Woolworths reintroduced purchase limits on toilet paper and paper towels nationwide as panic buying returns after re-emerging in Melbourne this week following a spike in COVID-19 cases.
"Another wild week for the domestic market," said Karen Jorritsma at RBC Capital Markets. "Expect these choppy conditions to continue and we now hit a blackout period for much of the Aussie corporates."
The strategist acknowledged that this week's market swings were made following a sharp uplift in shares since mid-March.
"Valuations are making everyone uncomfortable but sitting on the sidelines in a market that wants to rally is equally uncomfortable," she said.
Miners performed well over the week, with the sector rising 2.1 per cent.
Western Areas surged 22 per cent to $2.72 during the week after it reported striking significant nickel and copper-bearing sulphides during exploratory drilling at its Western Gawler project in South Australia.
Gold miner Saracen Mineral Holdings rose 13.6 per cent to $5.36, and Perseus Mining climbed 9.9 per cent to $1.22; Silver Lake Resources also climbed sharply, rising 8 per cent to $2.03. Elsewhere in the sector Sandfire Resources rallied 9.8 per cent to $5.14 during the week,
Another strong weekly gainer was AMP, rallying 6 per cent to $1.86. The financial services giant finally obtained approval to sell its life insurance division to Resolution Life
Unsurprisingly, investors also favoured companies that are relatively insulated from the economic cycle and healthcare companies were among the strongest performers for the week, with the sector up 1.4 per cent.
Fisher & Paykel rose 10.2 per cent to $29.63 and Resmed rose 7.4 per cent to $27.16.
But travel stocks were under pressure as investors faced the prospect of a slower-than-anticipated reopening of state and international borders.
Corporate Travel fell 18.9 per cent to $9.73, and Flight Centre dropped 16.8 per cent to $11.32, while Webjet shed 15.1 per cent to $3.38.
Qantas shares fell 9.1 per cent to $3.81 on Friday for a weekly loss of 12.8 per cent. The airline raised $1.36 billion from shareholders after issuing 372.7 million shares at $3.65 a share.
It will also sack 6000 employees as part of a three-year recovery plan as another 15,000 workers at Qantas remain stood down during the industrywide COVID-19 crisis.
Perenti Global tumbled 14.3 per cent to $1.19 after downgrading its earnings guidance. It now forecasts fiscal 2020 net profit of $106 million to $110 million, compared with previous $115 million - $120 million guidance.
