Former Thunder forward Malik Rose has been hired by the NBA as vice president of basketball operations, the league announced Monday.
Rose, a two-time NBA champion with the Spurs, played 20 games for the Thunder in the second half of the 2008-09 season. It was the final season of Rose's 13-year career.
Rose spent the last two seasons as assistant general manager for the Pistons. Former Thunder executive Troy Weaver was recently hired as Detroit's new general manager .
Rose, 45, holds a master's degree in sports management from Drexel, where he starred from 1992-96.
