Nasdaq's 'golden cross' joins Dow, S&P 500
chart flashed a "golden cross" pattern on Wednesday, to join the other major market indexes in producing the bullish trend pattern. A golden cross appears when the 50-day moving average crosses above the 200-day moving average, a technical event that many chart watchers believe marks the spot at shorter-term rally transitions to a longer-term uptrend. The Nasdaq's 50-DMA rose to 7,496.004 from 7,478.580 on Wednesday, while the 200-DMA edged up to 7,491.966 from 7,491.240.
