TOKYO (Kyodo) -- Tokyo shares extended their losses in afternoon trading Monday, with the benchmark Nikkei falling more than 2 percent to below the 22,000 mark, as investor sentiment was dented on a weekend spike in new coronavirus infections in the Japanese capital and elsewhere in the world.
