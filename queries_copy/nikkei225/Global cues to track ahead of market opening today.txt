Mumbai: Indian markets are likely to open lower on Monday tracking other Asian shares, which opened lower on Monday as virus fears resurface. Also, Wall Street seems set to extend last week’s sharp loss; Dow futures drop more than 300 points as of 7:15 am. Here are the global cues you need to track ahead of market opening.
Dow futures drop more than 300 points. Wall Street seems set to extend last week’s sharp loss. Stock futures fell on Sunday, pointing to more losses ahead. Investors grapple with signs of a second wave of coronavirus cases amid the reopening economy. Futures on the Dow dropped about 350 points. The S&P 500 and Nasdaq 100 futures were also down 1.4% and 1.0%, respectively
Stocks slip as virus fears resurface; China’s industrial production data lies ahead. Stocks in Asia declined Monday morning as fears resurface over the coronavirus. On the economic data front, Chinese economic data for May is set to be released around 10:00 a.m. HK/SIN, with industrial production as well as fixed asset investment for that month expected. In Japan, Nikkei 225 slipped 0.64% while the Topix index shed 0.33%. The Kospi dipped 0.68%, the S&P/ASX 200 in Australia slipped 0.33%. Overall, the MSCI Asia ex-Japan index traded 0.25% lower
Gold prices gained on Friday as investors bought the safe-haven metal amidst fears of a fresh wave of coronavirus cases. This was added to the gloomy economic outlook from the U.S. Federal Reserve. Spot gold was up 0.4% to $1,734.66 per ounce and was heading for its biggest gain since the week of April 10. US gold futures rose 0.2% to $1,742.80.
Oil posted it’s worst week since April, snapping a 6-week winning streak. Oil was little changed Friday, headed for a first weekly fall since April as new coronavirus cases spiked, stoking fears of a second wave of the virus hitting fuel demand.
West Texas Intermediate settled 8 cents lower at $36.26 per barrel, while Brent crude gained 18 cents to settle at $38.73.
