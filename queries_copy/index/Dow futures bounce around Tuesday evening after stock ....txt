U.S. stock-index futures on Tuesday swung between slight gains and losses in thinly traded action, after the broader market finished at about the lowest level in about two weeks. Futures for the Dow Jones Industrial Average YMM20 were last up around 56 points, or 0.2%, the S&P 500 index ESM20 rose 6.9 points, or 0.2%, and Nasdaq-100 futures NQM20 gained 23.5 points, or 0.3%,. After the regular session the U.S. Senate on Tuesday approved a nearly $500 billion aid package for small businesses and hospitals hit hard by the coronavirus pandemic, setting up a vote in the House of Representatives as President Donald Trump threw his support behind the measure. Trump, during a Tuesday evening news briefing centered on the country's response to the coronavirus epidemic, said that he would aim to ban immigration for a 60-day period, citing a need to focus on "American workers" after the pandemic hurt business activity.
