New Delhi: Aluminium prices on Thursday fell 65 paise to Rs 131.70 per kg as participants trimmed their positions amid a weak trend in spot market.
On the Multi Commodity Exchange , aluminium for delivery in November declined by 65 paise, or 0.49 per cent, to Rs 131.70 per kg in 2,906 lots.
Similarly, the metal for delivery in December weakened by 55 paise to Rs 133.85 per kg in 93 lots.
Analysts said cutting down of positions by participants on easing demand from consuming industries in physical market mainly kept aluminium prices lower here.
Nickel prices on Thursday drifted lower by Rs 10.90 to Rs 1,196.20 per kg in futures trade as speculators trimmed their positions owing to weak demand in spot market.
On the Multi Commodity Exchange, nickel for delivery in November declined by Rs 10.90, or 0.9 per cent, to Rs 1,196.20 per kg in 10,232 lots.
Similarly, the metal for delivery in December slumped by Rs 9.20, or 0.77 per cent, to Rs 1,192.20 per kg in 86 lots.
Analysts said, muted demand from alloy makers in spot market mainly weighed on nickel future prices here.
Copper futures on Thursday traded 0.32 per cent lower at Rs 442.40 per kg as investors reduced their bets, taking weak cues from global markets.
Traders said apart from subdued trend overseas, weak demand in spot market mainly pushed down copper prices.
On the Multi Commodity Exchange, copper contracts for November delivery shed Rs 1.40, or 0.32 per cent, to Rs 442.40 per kg in 6,916 lots.
Similarly, copper contracts for December delivery traded down by Rs 1.15, or 0.26 per cent, at Rs 447.30 per kg in 320 lots.
Globally, copper fell 0.33 per cent to USD 5,908 per tonne on the London Metal Exchange.
Zinc prices on Thursday fell 50 paise to Rs 188.55 per kg in futures trade as speculators cut bets, driven by easing demand in spot market.
On the Multi Commodity Exchange, zinc contracts for November delivery moved down by 50 paise, or 0.26 per cent, to Rs 188.55 per kg in 2,777 lots.
Similarly, the metal for delivery in December was trading lower by 75 paise, or 0.39 per cent, at Rs 190.05 per kg in 99 lots.
Analysts said offloading of positions by traders owing to slackened demand from consuming industries in physical market mainly weighed on zinc prices in futures trade.
