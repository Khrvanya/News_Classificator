Nickel prices on Monday edged up by Rs 1.80 to Rs 1,047.70 per kg in futures trade as speculators built positions owing to rising demand from alloy-makers in spot market.
On the Multi Commodity Exchange, nickel contracts for December delivery gained Rs 1.80, or 0.17 per cent, to Rs 1,047.70 per kg in a business turnover of 2,377 lots.
However, the metal for delivery in January traded lower by 20 paise, or 0.20 per cent, at Rs 1,044.80 per kg in a business turnover of 13 lots.
Copper futures on Monday traded 0.26 per cent lower at Rs 440.75 per kg in futures trade as speculators reduced their exposure on weak spot demand.
On the Multi Commodity Exchange, copper contracts for December delivery fell by Rs 1.15 or 0.26 per cent, to Rs 440.75 per kg in a business turnover of 18 lots.
Similarly, copper contracts for January delivery traded lower by 80 paise, or 0.18 per cent, at Rs 445.65 per kg in a business turnover of 1,194 lots.
Aluminium prices on Monday fell by Rs 1.75 to Rs 135.30 per kg in futures market as participants trimmed their positions amid a weak trend at spot market.
On the Multi Commodity Exchange, aluminium for delivery in December declined by Rs 1.75, or 1.28 per cent, to Rs 135.30 per kg in 6 lots.
Analysts said cutting down of positions by participants on easing demand from consuming industries in physical market mainly kept aluminium prices lower here.
Lead prices on Monday drifted lower by 0.03 per cent to Rs 151.05 per kg in futures trade as speculators cut bets on low demand.
On the Multi Commodity Exchange, lead for delivery in December fell by 5 paise, or 0.03 per cent, to Rs 151.05 per kg in 2 lots.
Analysts said off-loading of positions by participants owing to slackened demand from consuming industries in physical market mainly exerted pressure on lead prices here.
