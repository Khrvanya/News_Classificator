New Delhi: Copper futures on Friday fell 0.6 per cent to Rs 400.90 per kg as speculators reduced their exposure due to weak spot demand.
On the Multi Commodity Exchange, copper contracts for May delivery traded lower by Rs 2.40, or 0.6 per cent, at Rs 400.90 per kg in a business turnover of 2,282 lots.
Similarly, copper contracts for June delivery fell Rs 4.85, or 1.19 per cent, to Rs 403.60 per kg in a business turnover of 14 lots.
Traders attributed the fall in copper prices mainly to the subdued demand in spot market.
Nickel prices on Friday gained 0.19 per cent to Rs 930.80 per kg in futures trade as speculators built positions on rising demand from alloy-makers in spot market.
On the Multi Commodity Exchange, nickel contracts for May delivery gained Rs 1.80, or 0.19 per cent, to Rs 930.80 per kg in a business turnover of 720 lots.
Rising demand in spot market from alloy-makers mainly supported price rise in nickel futures, analysts said.
