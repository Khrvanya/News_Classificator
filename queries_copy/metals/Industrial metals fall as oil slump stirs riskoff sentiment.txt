The previous two sessions were the most turbulent days in the history of oil trading, as investors confronted the reality that worldwide supply will overwhelm demand for months or years and current production cuts are nowhere near sufficient.
Three-month copper on the London Metal Exchange (LME) was down 0.4% at $5,009 a tonne, as of 0240 GMT, while the most-traded June copper contract on the Shanghai Futures Exchange (ShFE) dropped 2.5% to 40,670 yuan ($5,740.54) a tonne.
LME aluminum dipped 0.1% to $1,489 a tonne, nickel dropped 2.6% to $11,885 a tonne, zinc was down 0.3% at $1,904.50 a tonne, lead decreased 0.6% to $1,655 a tonne and tin declined 0.5% to $14,690 a tonne.
In Shanghai, aluminum declined 0.1% to 12,120 yuan a tonne, nickel dropped 3.4% to 97,580 yuan a tonne, tin plunged 2.3% to 123,370 yuan a tonne and zinc fell 0.6% to 15,665 yuan a tonne.

* U.S. STIMULUS: The U.S. Senate on Tuesday approved $484 billion in fresh relief for the U.S. economy and hospitals hammered by the coronavirus pandemic, sending the measure to the House of Representatives for final passage later this week.
