Guar gum prices on Friday gained Rs 14 to Rs 6,660 per five quintal in futures trade as speculators widened their holdings on spot demand.
On the National Commodity and Derivatives Exchange, guar gum contracts for February delivery traded higher by 0.21 per cent, or Rs 14, to Rs 6,660 per five quintal with an open interest of 1,275 lots.
Likewise, the delivery of March contracts surged by Rs 39, or 0.58 per cent, to Rs 6,759 per five quintal as open interest stood at 55,140 lots.
Analysts said after tracking firm physical market trend traders raised their bets which led to rise in guar gum prices.
Refined soya oil prices on Friday rose 0.02 per cent to Rs 841.6 per 10 kg in futures trade as speculators raised their bets.
On the National Commodity and Derivatives Exchange, refined soya oil for February delivery moved up by 20 paise, or 0.02 per cent, to Rs 841.6 per 10 kg in 14,830 lots.
Similarly, refined soya oil contracts for March delivery edged up by 20 paise, or 0.02 per cent, to Rs 827 per 10 kg in 54,030 lots
Analysts said widening of positions by traders mainly helped refined soya oil prices to trade higher in futures market here.
Soybean prices on Friday rose by Rs 38 to Rs 4,010 per quintal in futures market as traders created fresh positions in line with spot demand.
On the National Commodity and Derivatives Exchange, soybean for February delivery rose by Rs 38, or 0.96 per cent, to Rs 4,010 per quintal with an open interest of 7,950 lots.
Soybean for March delivery rose by Rs 30, or 0.77 per cent, to Rs 3,940 per quintal with an open interest of 1,43,605 lots.
Market players said raising of fresh positions by speculators on the back of higher spot demand led to rise in soybean prices.
Mustard seed prices on Friday dropped by Rs 33 to Rs 4,022 per quintal in futures trade as speculators cut down their positions on subdued demand.
On the National Commodity and Derivatives Exchange, mustard seed contracts for February delivery fell by Rs 33, or 0.74 per cent, to Rs 4,022 per quintal in a business turnover of 2,040 lots.
Marketmen said offloading of positions by participants following a weak trend in spot market mainly led to fall in mustard seed prices.

On the National Commodity and Derivatives Exchange, guar seed for February contracts rose by Rs 6, or 0.16 per cent, to Rs 3,824 per 10 quintal with an open interest of 5,290 lots.
Similarly, guar seed for March delivery gained Rs 6, or 0.16 per cent to Rs 3,860 per 10 quintal with an open interest lots of 72,625.
According to marketmen, raising of bets by speculators tracking a firm trend in spot market on thin supplies from growing belts mainly led to rise in guar seed prices here.
Cottonseed oil cake prices on Friday traded up by Rs 26 to Rs 1,805 per quintal in futures trade after speculators created fresh positions on spot demand.

Coriander prices on Friday fell by Rs 10 to Rs 6,191 per quintal in futures trade as speculators reduced their positions amid weak demand at spot market.
On the Multi Commodity Exchange, coriander contracts for April eased by Rs 10, or 0.16 per cent, to Rs 6,191 per quintal in 7,090 lots.
Similarly, the spice for May delivery traded lower by Rs 21, or 0.34 per cent, at Rs 6,245 per quintal in 910 lots.
Market analysts said subdued demand in spot market mainly led to decline in coriander prices here.
