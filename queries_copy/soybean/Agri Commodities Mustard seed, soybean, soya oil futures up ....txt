New Delhi: Mustard seed prices were up by 0.51 per cent to Rs 3,938 per quintal in futures trade Tuesday as participants widened their holdings tracking a firm trend at the spot market.
On the National Commodity and Derivatives Exchange, mustard seed contracts for August rose by Rs 20, or 0.51 per cent, to Rs 3,938 per quintal with an open interest of 78,320 lots.
Mustard seed for September delivery also traded higher by same margin at Rs 3,958 per quintal in 19,660 lots.
Marketmen said strong demand from oil millers mainly pushed up mustard seed prices here.
Coriander prices fell 3.99 per cent to Rs 6,539 per quintal in futures market Tuesday as participants cut down positions amid muted domestic and export demand.
Besides, rising arrivals from major producing belts and profit booking at higher levels weighed on the sentiment.
At the National Commodity and Derivatives Exchange, coriander for delivery in August contracts declined by Rs 272, or 3.99 per cent, to Rs 6,539 per quintal with an open interest of 22,170 lots.
Similarly, coriander for September contracts delivery was trading lower by Rs 275, or 3.99 per cent, at Rs 6,619 per quintal with an open interest of 14,760 lots.
Analysts attributed the fall in coriander futures to subdued demand in the physical market against adequate stock position on increased supplies from producing regions.

Guar gum prices fell by Rs 28 to Rs 8,422 per five quintal in futures trade Tuesday as operators cut down their positions amid low demand.
Marketmen said trimming of bets by participants, following a weak trend at the spot market due to subdued export demand from oil drilling industries, pulled down guar gum prices.
On the National Commodity and Derivatives Exchange, guar gum contracts for August delivery traded lower by Rs 28, or 0.33 per cent, at Rs 8,422 per five quintal with an open interest of 48,600 lots.
Guar gum contracts for September delivery also shed Rs 29, or 0.34 per cent, to Rs 8,555 per five quintal, mirroring an open interest of 17,750 lots.
Guar seed prices fell by Rs 4.5 to Rs 4,213 per 10 quintal in futures trade Tuesday on liquidated holdings by operators in line with a weak trend at the spot market.
Marketmen attributed the fall in guar seed to low demand amid increasing supplies from growing regions in the spot market.
On the National Commodity and Derivatives Exchange, guar seed delivery for August contracts fell by Rs 4.5, or 0.11 per cent, to Rs 4,213 per 10 quintal depicting an open interest of 93,740 lots.
Similarly, the seed for delivery in July contracts shed Rs 7, or 0.16 per cent, to Rs 4,272.5 per 10 quintal with an open interest of 25,670 lots.
Soybean prices rose 0.67 per cent to Rs 3,604 per quintal in futures trade Tuesday after traders raised their bets on spot demand.
Marketmen said widening of bets by investors taking positive cues from global markets mainly gave push to soybean prices at futures trade here.
On the National Commodity and Derivatives Exchange, soybean contracts for July delivery went up by Rs 24, or 0.67 per cent, to Rs 3,604 per quintal with an open interest of 67,240 lots.
Similarly, soybean contracts for August edged up by Rs 17, or 0.47 per cent, to Rs 3,623 per quintal with an open interest of 21,230 lots.
Refined soya oil prices rose 0.32 per cent to Rs 730.55 per 10 kg in futures trade Tuesday as speculators created fresh positions.
On the National Commodity and Derivatives Exchange, refined soya oil contracts for August delivery went up by Rs 2.3, or 0.32 per cent, to Rs 730.55 per 10 kg with an open interest of 42,190 lots.
Similarly, the oil for September delivery traded higher by Rs 2.4, or 0.33 per cent, at Rs 727.5 per 10 kg in 18,280 lots.
Analysts said fresh positions built up by traders against restricted arrivals from growing regions mainly led to the rise in refined soya oil prices.
