Coriander prices on Wednesday climbed Rs 206 to Rs 6,440 per quintal in futures trade on firm domestic demand and restricted supplies from producing belts.
On the National Commodity and Derivatives Exchange, coriander for January delivery shot up by Rs 206, or 3.3 per cent, to Rs 6,440 per quintal with an open interest of 7,080 lots.
Similarly, the spice for April delivery traded higher by Rs 237, or 3.48 per cent, at Rs 7,049 per quintal in 8,770 lots.
A firm trend in spot market and restricted supplies from producing regions pushed up coriander prices here, market analysts said.
Cottonseed oil cake prices on Wednesday fell by Rs 2 to Rs 2,093 per quintal in futures trade as participants reduced their bets amid a weak trend in spot market.
Market players said sell-off by participants at the existing levels amid subdued trend in market mainly weighed on cottonseed oil cake prices.
On the National Commodity and Derivatives Exchange, cottonseed oil cake to be delivered in January fell by Rs 2, or 0.10 per cent, to Rs 2,093 per quintal with an open interest of 38,380 lots.
Cottonseed oil cake for delivery in February went down by Rs 2, or 0.09 per cent, to Rs 2,119 per quintal in 69,470 lots.
Guar gum prices on Wednesday dropped by Rs 16 to Rs 7,340 per five quintal in futures market as traders offloaded their positions amid subdued demand.
Marketmen said trimming of positions by participants amid weak demand dragged down guar gum prices here.
On the National Commodity and Derivatives Exchange, guar gum contracts for January delivery slipped by Rs 16, or 0.22 per cent, to Rs 7,340 per five quintal in 11,045 lots.
Guar gum contracts for February delivery eased by Rs 12, or 0.16 per cent, to Rs 7,432 per five quintal in 55,595 lots.
Guar seed prices on Wednesday rose by Rs 6 to Rs 4,104 per 10 quintal in futures trade after participants widened their holdings driven by a firm trend in spot market.
On the National Commodity and Derivatives Exchange, guar seed for January contracts rose by Rs 6, or 0.15 per cent, to Rs 4,104 per 10 quintal with an open interest of 32,780 lots.
Similarly, guar seed for February delivery gained Rs 8 to Rs 4,154 per 10 quintal with an open interest lots of 69,805.
According to marketmen, raising of bets by speculators tracking a firm trend in spot market on thin supplies from growing belts mainly led to rise in guar seed prices here.
Mustard seed prices on Wednesday rose by Rs 42 to Rs 4,505 per quintal in futures trade as speculators raised their bets amid limited supplies.
Marketmen said apart from restricted arrivals from growing belts demand from oil mills in spot market mainly lifted mustard seed prices here.
On the National Commodity and Derivatives Exchange, mustard seed for delivery in January rose by Rs 42, or 0.94 per cent, to Rs 4,505 per quintal with an open interest of 10,450 lots.
Similarly, the delivery for February gained Rs 40, or 0.90 per cent, to Rs 4,504 per quintal with an open interest of 10,730 lots.
Refined soya oil prices on Wednesday rose marginally 0.04 per cent to Rs 917 per 10 kg in futures trade as speculators raised their bets.
On the National Commodity and Derivatives Exchange, refined soya oil for January delivery moved up by 40 paise, or 0.04 per cent, to Rs 917 per 10 kg in 22,775 lots.
Similarly, refined soya oil contracts for February delivery edged up by 45 paise, or 0.04 per cent, to Rs 915.4 per 10 kg in 52,210 lots.
Analysts said widening of positions by traders mainly helped refined soya oil prices to trade higher in futures market here.
Soybean prices on Wednesday rose by Rs 32 to Rs 4,228 per quintal in futures market as traders created fresh positions on strong spot demand.
On the National Commodity and Derivatives Exchange, soybean for January delivery rose Rs 32, or 0.76 per cent, to Rs 4,228 per quintal with an open interest of 44,460 lots.
Soybean for February delivery also rose by Rs 32, or 0.77 per cent, to Rs 4,242 per quintal with an open interest of 1,99,265 lots.
Market players said raising of fresh positions by speculators on the back of higher demand mainly led to the rise in soybean prices.
