New Delhi: Mustard seed prices on Friday dropped Rs 16 to Rs 4,055 per quintal in futures trade as participants reduced their holdings on sluggish demand.
On the National Commodity and Derivatives Exchange, mustard seed contracts for April delivery fell by Rs 16, or 0.39 per cent, to Rs 4,055 per quintal in a business turnover of 16,120 lots.
Mustard seed contracts for delivery in May declined by Rs 19, or 0.47 per cent, to Rs 4,068 per quintal in a business turnover of 5,620 lots.
Marketmen said offloading of positions by participants following a weak trend in spot market mainly led to fall in mustard seed prices.
Soybean prices on Friday fell 0.11 to Rs 3,744 per quintal in futures market as participants cut down their positions on weak spot demand.
On the National Commodity and Derivatives Exchange, soybean to be delivered in April contracts moved down by Rs 4, or 0.11 per cent, to Rs 3,744 per quintal with an open interest of 1,16,815 lots.
Soybean for delivery in May contracts also eased by Rs 10, or 0.27 per cent, to Rs 3,740 per quintal with an open interest of 62,265 lots.
The fall in soybean prices in futures trade was mostly due to reduction of exposure by participants, marketmen said.
Refined soya oil prices on Friday fell 0.20 per cent to Rs 798.4 per 10 kg in futures trade as speculators reduced holdings amid subdued demand at spot market.
On the National Commodity and Derivatives Exchange, refined soya oil for delivery in March declined by Rs 1.6, or 0.20 per cent, to Rs 798.4 per 10 kg in 23,315 lots.
The refined soya oil contracts for April dropped by Rs 5.6, or 0.71 per cent, to Rs 782 per 10 kg in 29,535 lots.
Market analysts said cutting down of positions by participants against ample stocks mainly influenced refined soya oil prices.
Guar gum prices on Friday slumped Rs 115 to Rs 6,264 per five quintal in futures market as traders trimmed their holdings in line with weak trend at the spot market.
On the National Commodity and Derivatives Exchange, guar gum for March delivery fell by Rs 115, or 1.8 per cent, to Rs 6,264 per five quintal in 11,045 lots.
Guar gum for April delivery eased by Rs 114, or 1.77 per cent, to Rs 6,341 per five quintal in 46,565 lots.
Marketmen said trimming of positions by participants amid weak demand mainly dragged down guar gum prices here.
Guar seed prices on Friday fell by Rs 20 to Rs 3,806 per 10 quintal in futures trade as participants reduced their holdings in line with a weak spot demand.
Marketmen attributed the downward trend in guar seed prices to increasing supplies from growing regions.
On the National Commodity and Derivatives Exchange, guar seed contracts for March delivery fell by Rs 20, or 0.52 per cent, to Rs 3,806 per 10 quintal with an open interest of 28,785 lots.
Similarly, guar seed contracts for April delivery shed Rs 30, or 0.79 per cent, to Rs 3,790 per 10 quintal with an open interest of 61,815 lots.
Cottonseed oil cake prices on Friday traded up by Rs 59 to Rs 1,866 per quintal in futures trade as speculators created fresh positions driven by firm spot demand.
On the National Commodity and Derivatives Exchange, cottonseed oil cake for delivery in March traded higher by Rs 59, or 3.27 per cent, to Rs 1,866 per quintal with an open interest of 44,520 lots.
Likewise for delivery in April, cottonseed oil cake gained Rs 53, or 2.9 per cent, to Rs 1,883 per quintal with an open interest of 57,500 lots.
Marketmen said raising of positions by participants amid a rising demand from cattle-feed makers mainly influenced cottonseed oil cake prices here.
Coriander prices on Friday rose by Rs 18 to Rs 6,276 per quintal in futures trade on positive demand in spot market.
On the National Commodity and Derivatives Exchange, coriander for April delivery shot up by Rs 18, or 0.29 per cent, to Rs 6,276 per quintal with an open interest of 6,400 lots.
Similarly, the spice for May delivery traded higher by Rs 109, or 1.77 per cent, at Rs 6,275 per quintal in 1,670 lots.
A firm trend in spot market and restricted supplies from producing regions pushed coriander prices up, market analysts said.
