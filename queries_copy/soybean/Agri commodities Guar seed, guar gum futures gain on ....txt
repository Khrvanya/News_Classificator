New Delhi: Guar seed prices on Thursday rose by Rs 52 to Rs 3,636 per 10 quintal in futures trade after speculators increased their positions driven by a firm trend at spot market.
On the National Commodity and Derivatives Exchange, guar seed for April contracts increased by Rs 52, or 1.45 per cent, to Rs 3,636 per 10 quintal with an open interest of 1,670 lots.
Similarly, guar seed for May delivery gained Rs 8, or 0.22 per cent, to Rs 3,622 per 10 quintal with an open interest of 43,060 lots.
According to marketmen, raising of bets by speculators tracking a firm trend in spot market on thin supplies from growing belts mainly led to rise in guar seed prices here.
Cottonseed oil cake prices on Thursday rose by Rs 31 to Rs 2,030 per quintal in futures trade as speculators increased their holdings on spot demand.
On the National Commodity and Derivatives Exchange, cottonseed oil cake to be delivered in April surged by Rs 31, or 1.55 per cent, to Rs 2,030 per quintal with an open interest 2,530 lots.
Cottonseed oil cake for delivery in May went up by Rs 28, or 1.4 per cent, to Rs 2,034 per quintal in 57,590 lots.
Analysts said after tracking firm physical market trend, traders raised their bets which led to rise in cottonseed oil cake prices.

Coriander prices on Thursday increased by Rs 25 to Rs 6,450 per quintal in futures trade on spot demand and restricted supplies from producing belts.
On the National Commodity and Derivatives Exchange, coriander for April delivery shot up by Rs 25, or 0.39 per cent, to Rs 6,450 per quintal with an open interest of 1,130 lots.
Following the upward trend, the spice for May delivery traded higher by Rs 67, or 1.06 per cent, at Rs 6,360 per quintal in 4,360 lots.
A firm trend in spot market and restricted supplies from producing regions pushed up coriander prices here, market analysts said.
