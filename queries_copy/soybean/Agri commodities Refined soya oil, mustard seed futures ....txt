New Delhi: Refined soya oil prices on Friday rose 0.26 per cent to Rs 776 per 10 kg in futures trade as speculators raised their bets.
On the National Commodity and Derivatives Exchange, refined soya oil for March delivery moved up by Rs 2, or 0.26 per cent, to Rs 776 per 10 kg in 15,260 lots.
Similarly, refined soya oil contracts for April delivery edged up by Rs 13.8, or 1.85 per cent, to Rs 760.6 per 10 kg in 41,980 lots.
Analysts said widening of positions by traders mainly helped refined soya oil prices to trade higher in futures market.
Mustard seed prices on Friday rose by Rs 37 to Rs 3,975 per quintal in futures trade as participants widened their bets amid firm spot demand.
On the National Commodity and Derivatives Exchange, mustard seed for delivery in April rose by Rs 37, or 0.94 per cent, to Rs 3,975 per quintal with an open interest of 15,720 lots.
Similarly, the delivery for May went up by Rs 49, or 1.25 per cent, to Rs 3,960 per quintal with an open interest of 15,300 lots.
Marketmen said apart from restricted arrivals from growing belts, demand from oil mills in spot market mainly lifted mustard seed prices here.
Guar seed prices rose by Rs 54 to Rs 3,444 per 10 quintal in futures trade on Friday due to raising of bets by participants following firm spot demand.
Marketmen said apart from fresh bets, gain in guar seed futures prices was also due to limited supplies from growing belts.
On the National Commodity and Derivatives Exchange, guar seed contracts for April delivery rose by Rs 54, or 1.59 per cent, to Rs 3,444 per 10 quintal with an open interest of 49,310 lots.
Similarly, guar seed contracts for May delivery traded up by Rs 44, or 1.29 per cent, to Rs 3,460 per 10 quintal in an open interest of 9,880 lots.
Guar gum prices on Friday gained Rs 96 to Rs 5,240 per five quintal in futures trade as speculators increased their holdings on spot demand.
On the National Commodity and Derivatives Exchange, guar gum contracts for March delivery traded higher 1.87 per cent, or Rs 96, to Rs 5,240 per five quintal with an open interest of 175 lots.
Likewise, the contracts for delivery in April surged by Rs 150, or 2.89 per cent, to Rs 5,335 per five quintal as open interest stood at 41,385 lots.
Analysts said after tracking firm physical market trend, traders raised their bets which led to rise in guar gum prices.
Coriander prices on Friday rose by Rs 10 to Rs 5,660 per quintal in futures trade on spot demand.
On the National Commodity and Derivatives Exchange, coriander for April delivery shot up by Rs 10, or 0.18 per cent, to Rs 5,660 per quintal with an open interest of 5,730 lots.
Similarly, the spice for May delivery traded higher by Rs 51, or 0.92 per cent, at Rs 5,602 per quintal in 2,980 lots.
A firm trend in spot market and restricted supplies from producing regions pushed up coriander prices here, market analysts said.
