New Delhi: Coriander prices rose by Rs 60 to Rs 5,650 per quintal in futures market on Tuesday as participants built up fresh positions on firm spot demand.
Besides, tight stocks from restricted arrivals in major growing belts aided the rally in coriander futures, analysts said.
At the National Commodity and Derivatives Exchange, coriander for October delivery rose by Rs 60, or 1.07 per cent, to Rs 5,650 per quintal with an open interest of 10,620 lots.
Similarly, delivery for November was trading higher by Rs 21, or 0.37 per cent, to Rs 5,712 per quintal with an open interest of 11,280 lots.
Cottonseed oil cake prices fell by Rs 9 at Rs 2,126 per quintal in futures trade on Tuesday as participants reduced their bets amid weak trend at the spot market.
Market players said sell-off by participants at the existing levels amid subdued trend at the market mainly weighed on cottonseed oil cake prices.
On the National Commodity and Derivatives Exchange, cottonseed oil cake to be delivered in December fell by Rs 9, or 0.42 per cent, to Rs 2,126 per quintal in an open interest of 45,670 lots.
Cottonseed oil cake for delivery in January next year went down by Rs 18, or 0.86 per cent, to Rs 2,069 per quintal in 17,950 lots.
Guar seed prices rose by 5 paise to Rs 3,943 per 10 quintal in futures trade on Tuesday due to raising of bets by participants following firm spot demand.
Marketmen said the gain in guar seed futures prices was mainly due to limited supplies from growing belts.
On the National Commodity and Derivatives Exchange, guar seed contracts for October delivery rose by 5 paise, or 0.01 per cent, to Rs 3,943 per 10 quintal in an open interest of 43,030 lots.
Similarly, guar seed contracts for November delivery traded up by Rs 2, or 0.05 per cent, to Rs 3,985 per 10 quintal in an open interest of 47,170 lots.
Mustard seed prices eased by Rs 9 to Rs 3,945 per quintal in futures trade on Tuesday as speculators cut down their positions on subdued demand.
At the National Commodity and Derivatives Exchange, mustard seed contracts for October delivery fell by Rs 9, or 0.23 per cent, to Rs 3,945 per quintal in a business turnover of 28,130 lots.
Similarly, mustard seed contracts for November delivery declined by Rs 8, or 0.20 per cent, to Rs 4,010 per quintal in a business turnover of 34,190 lots.
Marketmen said offloading of positions by participants following a weak trend at the spot market due to easing demand led to fall in mustard seed prices.
Guar gum prices declined by Rs 12 to Rs 7,473 per five quintal in futures trade on Tuesday amid tepid export demand at the spot market.
On the National Commodity and Derivatives Exchange, guar gum contracts for November delivery traded lower by Rs 12, or 0.16 per cent, at Rs 7,473 per five quintal with an open interest of 20,905 lots.
Guar gum contracts for December delivery also traded lower by Rs 16, or 0.21 per cent, to Rs 7,590 per five quintal mirroring an open interest of 45,945 lots.
Soybean futures prices advanced by Rs 27 to Rs 3,939 per quintal in futures trade on Tuesday as participants raised their bets.
On the National Commodity and Derivatives Exchange, soybean for delivery in October contracts rose by Rs 27, or 0.69 per cent, to Rs 3,939 per quintal clocking an open interest of 32,110 lots.
The contracts for November delivery traded higher by Rs 6, or 0.16 per cent, to Rs 3,765 per quintal in an open interest of 68,010 lots.
Marketmen said raising of holdings by participants amid increase in demand lifted soybean prices in futures trade here.
Refined soya oil prices traded higher by 5 paise at Rs 765 per 10 kg in futures trade on Tuesday as speculators raised their holdings at the spot market.
On the National Commodity and Derivatives Exchange, refined soya oil contracts for October delivery traded up by Rs 5 paise, or 0.07 per cent, at Rs 765 per 10 kg with an open interest of 31,970 lots.
Similarly, refined soya oil for November delivery advanced by Rs 2.05, or 0.27 per cent, to Rs 760.85 per 10 kg in 15,120 lots.
Market analysts said positions built up by participants against reduced stocks, mainly influenced refined soya oil prices here.
