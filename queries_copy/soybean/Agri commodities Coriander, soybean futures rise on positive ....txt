New Delhi: Coriander prices on Wednesday rose by Rs 139 to Rs 6,286 per quintal in futures trade on spot demand and restricted supplies from producing belts.
On the National Commodity and Derivatives Exchange, coriander for April delivery shot up by Rs 139, or 2.26 per cent, to Rs 6,286 per quintal with an open interest of 2,400 lots.
Similarly, the spice for May delivery traded higher by Rs 110, or 1.84 per cent, at Rs 6.090 per quintal in 3,530 lots.
A firm trend in spot market and restricted supplies from producing regions pushed coriander prices up, market analysts said.
Soybean prices on Wednesday rose by Rs 2 to Rs 3,792 per quintal in futures market as traders created fresh positions tracking positive spot demand.
On the National Commodity and Derivatives Exchange, soybean for April delivery rose by Rs 2, or 0.05 per cent, to Rs 3,792 per quintal with an open interest of 61,030 lots.
Soybean for May delivery went up by Rs 36, or 0.96 per cent, to Rs 3,776 per quintal with an open interest of 80,825 lots.
Market players said raising of fresh positions by speculators on the back of higher demand mainly led to rise in soybean prices.
Refined soya oil prices on Wednesday went up by 0.42 per cent to Rs 814.4 per 10 kg in futures trade as speculators raised their bets.
On the National Commodity and Derivatives Exchange, refined soya oil for April delivery moved up by Rs 3.4, or 0.42 per cent, to Rs 814.4 per 10 kg in 31,775 lots.
Similarly, refined soya oil contracts for May delivery edged up by Rs 4.2, or 0.54 per cent, to Rs 788.4 per 10 kg in 15,700 lots.
Analysts said widening of positions by traders mainly helped refined soya oil prices to trade higher in futures market.
Guar gum prices on Wednesday gained Rs 69 to Rs 5,330 per five quintal in futures trade as speculators increased their holdings on spot demand.
On the National Commodity and Derivatives Exchange, guar gum contracts for April delivery traded higher 1.31 per cent, or Rs 69, to Rs 5,330 per five quintal with an open interest of 9,165 lots.
Likewise, the contracts for delivery in May rose by Rs 96, or 1.81 per cent, to Rs 5,392 per five quintal as open interest stood at 38,040 lots.
Analysts said after tracking firm physical market trend, traders raised their bets which led to rise in guar gum prices.
Guar seed prices on Wednesday rose by Rs 14 to Rs 3,416 per 10 quintal in futures trade after speculators increased their positions driven by a firm trend at spot market.
On the National Commodity and Derivatives Exchange, guar seed for April contracts increased by Rs 14, or 0.41 per cent, to Rs 3,416 per 10 quintal with an open interest of 28,270 lots.
Similarly, guar seed for May delivery gained Rs 4, or 0.12 per cent, to Rs 3,456 per 10 quintal with an open interest of 30,780 lots.
According to marketmen, raising of bets by speculators tracking a firm trend in spot market on thin supplies from growing belts mainly led to rise in guar seed prices here.
