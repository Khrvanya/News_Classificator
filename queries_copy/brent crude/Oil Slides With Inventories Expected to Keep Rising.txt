Prices have fallen despite agreement by OPEC, allies to curb output; crude is down 67% this year
Oil prices fell Tuesday, dropping back toward an 18-year low with inventories set to continue rising despite recent supply cuts.
U.S. crude futures slid 10% to $20.11 a barrel on the New York Mercantile Exchange, recording a third consecutive drop and falling back toward their 18-year low of $20.09 from March 30. Prices are down 67% this year. Brent crude, the global gauge of oil prices, fell 6.7% to $29.60 a barrel on the Intercontinental Exchange.
