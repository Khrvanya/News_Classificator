Sugar stocks rallied on Tuesday as reports suggested a possible global shortage of the commodity this year. As many as five stocks jumped more than 10 per cent.
Meanwhile, HCC jumped 4 per cent after the company received a significant order from Delhi Metro. All the sectoral indices on NSE ended the day in the green.
Equity benchmarks BSE Sensex rose 192 points to 40,869 while Nifty 60 points to 12,052.
"Iran is under pressure from world leaders not to escalate the issue further. Oil prices cooled as Iran stayed away from retaliation. The first advance estimates for FY20 GDP by MoS is expected at 5 per cent, which is already factored by the market,” said Vinod Nair, Head of Research, Geojit Financial Services.
Here is a lowdown of the movers and shakers of Tuesday’s session on Dalal Street:
Shares of Hindustan Construction Company (HCC) rallied 3.75 per cent to Rs 10.50 after its joint venture company received a Rs 489 crore contract from Delhi Metro Rail Corporation under Phase IV. The contract is for design and construction of 2.03-km long twin tunnels on the Janakpuri West-RK Ashram corridor.
Shares of ITI rallied 13.37 per cent to Rs 103.90 after the company’s consolidated net profit jumped to Rs 168.25 crore for December quarter. It had posted a net profit of Rs 13.58 crore in the year-ago period. The net worth of the company turned positive for the first time in 16 years.
Shares of Avenue Supermarts jumped 1.42 per cent to Rs 1,815.15 on BSE after the company allotted listed commercial papers worth Rs 100 crore with maturity at February 25, 2020.
An accident at PI Industries’ Gujarat unit killed 2 people and left 9 injured, causing shares of the company to slip 0.81 per cent to Rs 1,407.90 on BSE. In last one year, the stock has gained 64 per cent.
Most sugar stocks jumped up to 20 per cent after media reports of a global sugar shortage this year. KM Sugar gained 20 per cent while Piccadily Agro added 17.5 per cent. Rajshree Sugars, KCP Sugars and Magath Sugar added between 10-12 per cent on BSE.
HDFC Bank and Reliance Industries were among the biggest contributors to Sensex’s nearly 200-point jump. They together added 150 points. Other major contributors were HDFC and Kotak Mahindra Bank. ICICI Bank and Infosys together shed 70 points.
Over 200 stocks hit their upper circuit on BSE as market rebounded from Monday’s massive fall. They included Centrum Capital, Suzlon Energy, Adani Green, Indiabulls Integrated Services, Dishman Carbogen Amcis and DHFL, among others.
Smallcap stocks were on the forefront of the rally. Nifty Smallcap index added 1.21 per cent to 5,900, while Nifty Midcap rose 0.69 per cent to 17,021 and Nifty500 gained 0.6 per cent to 9,805.
SBI was the most active stock on NSE in terms of turnover, followed by Reliance Industries, Indiabulls Housing Finance, HDFC Bank and HDFC. In terms of the volume of transactions, YES Bank topped the charts. It was followed by Vodafone Idea, SBI, GMR Infra and Dish TV.
Over 50 stocks turned ‘overbought’ as they crossed the 70 mark on Relative Strength Index (RSI). They include Centrum Capital, Mawana Sugars, Dhampur Sugar, Swan Energy, Balrampur Chini Mills, MCX, Zuari Agro and Apex Frozen Foods, among others.
