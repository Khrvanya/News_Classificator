US markets took a bit of a breather on Friday for Independence Day on July 4. However, US stock futures traded slightly higher on Sunday night as Wall St tried to build on the momentum from last week's performance. The Dow and S&P 500 rose 3.3% and 4% last week while the Nasdaq advanced 4.6% in that time.  Dow futures rose 95 points, or 0.4%, S&P 500 futures gained 0.3%, and Nasdaq-100 futures advanced 0.4%.

Stocks in Asia Pacific were mixed in Monday morning trade as the WHO reported over the weekend a record high number of coronavirus cases in 24 hours. Ignoring this data, stocks in Japan advanced with the Nikkei 225 rising 0.36% in early trade. Shares of Softbank Group jumped about 1.5%. The Topix index added 0.45% while the South Korean Kospi advanced 0.58%. The Australian S&P/ASX 200 slipped 0.15% and the MSCI Asia ex-Japan index traded 0.05% higher.
Oil prices have fallen significantly as growing coronavirus cases stoke fuel demand worries. The big question now lies as to whether both derivatives will once again fall below the 40$ mark. Brent crude futures were down 37 cents, or 0.9%, at $42.77 a barrel. West Texas Intermediate crude futures fell 34 cents, or 0.8%, to $40.31 a barrel. Both benchmarks rose more than 2% on Thursday where Prices were supported by stronger-than-expected US jobs data and a fall in US crude inventories. For last week, Brent was up 4.3% and WTI is up 4.7%
Gold was flat on Friday trading in a narrow $4 range. This is despite the fact that the precious metal has recorded a stupendous rally in decent times. Investors and analysts expect that precious metals including singer will continue to surge higher as the equity markets get more and more inflated. Worries over covid cases and trade tensions between the US and China overshadowed strong US jobs data. Spot gold was unchanged at $1,775.12 per ounce while US gold futures eased 0.1% to $1,787.80.
Virus concerns quelled optimism over upbeat data for EU stocks last week. European stocks closed lower on Friday with The Stoxx 600 closed down around 0.9%. In terms of sectors, Basic resources fell 1.7% to lead losses while Travel and leisure shares bucked the downward trend rising 0.1%
